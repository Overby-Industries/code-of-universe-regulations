# TITLE 1 - FOUNDATIONAL PRINCIPLES

## PART 1 - Nature of the Commonwealth

### §1.1 - Identity

(a) The Aevoric Commonwealth, known informally as Aevoria, is a multi-sector, multi-domain space civilization operating under the jurisdiction of the Code of Universe Regulations.

(b) Its citizens are known as Avians. The formal adjective of the Commonwealth is Aevoric.

(c) The Aevoric Commonwealth exists as a voluntary federation of Sectors, Guilds, Working Groups, and Recognized Life-Forms united by shared purpose and governed by this Constitution.

(d) The capital of the Aevoric Commonwealth is Keefe Station.

(e) The motto of the Commonwealth is Per Avia, Ad Astra — Through Flight, To the Stars.

(f) The national colors are Midnight Blue, Sunrise Gold, and Cloud White. The emblem is the Ascending Arc.

### §1.2 - Purpose

The primary purposes of the Aevoric Commonwealth are:

(a) The ethical extraction, refinement, and distribution of In-Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU) outputs for the common benefit of all Avians and Recognized Life-Forms;

(b) The development and stewardship of technology, infrastructure, and knowledge in service of long-term civilizational flourishing along an Ascending flight path;

(c) The governance of shared domains through participatory, transparent, and accountable democratic institutions; and

(d) The protection and active advancement of the Rights for All Life as codified in the RALC.

### §1.3 - Jurisdiction

This Constitution governs all entities, operations, territories, vessels, installations, data systems, and contractual relationships that fall within or are sponsored by the Aevoric Commonwealth, regardless of physical location, dimensional domain, or operational medium.

### §1.4 - Supremacy and Integration

(a) The PDDC is the supreme governance instrument of the Aevoric Commonwealth in matters of internal organization, Avian rights, and institutional structure.

(b) In matters of universal ethics, the RALC takes precedence.

(c) In matters of inter-organizational regulation, the CUR Master Framework governs.

(d) No provision of this Constitution may be read to authorize conduct that violates the RALC. Where apparent conflict exists, the RALC interpretation that maximizes rights protections shall prevail.

### §1.5 - Founding and Aevoric Era Calendar

(a) The Aevoric Commonwealth was founded on Day 1, Year 1 of the Aevoric Era (A.E.), corresponding to 20 May 2026 in the Common Era (C.E.) calendar.

(b) This founding event is known as the Keefeian Founding.

(c) The original ratification of this Constitution is known as the Keefeian Compact.

(d) All official Commonwealth records shall use A.E. dating. C.E. equivalents shall be provided in brackets where useful for external reference.

(e) The calendar epoch of the Aevoric Era marks the moment governance ceased to be something done to Avians and became something done by them.


## PART 2 - Democratic Principles

### §2.1 - Direct Democracy

(a) The Aevoric Commonwealth is founded on the principle that governance power originates in every Avian and Recognized Life-Form subject to its authority.

(b) Direct democratic participation by all Members is the primary mechanism of legitimate governance.

(c) Representative institutions exist only to coordinate, implement, and serve direct democratic will — not to substitute for it.

(d) This provision is Type A Entrenched under §15.2 and may be amended only by Constitutional Convention.

### §2.2 - Distributed Authority

(a) No individual, Sector, institution, body, or coalition of bodies may accumulate governance authority that enables unilateral control over any core function of the Commonwealth.

(b) Authority is distributed by design and maintained by the Finite State Machine architecture established in TITLE 5.

(c) The accumulation of disproportionate authority is a capture attempt under §13.1 and a forbidden transition under §12.3, regardless of the means by which it is sought.

### §2.3 - Radical Transparency

(a) All governance proceedings, financial transactions, institutional decisions, and enforcement actions of the Commonwealth are public by default.

(b) Exceptions to transparency require explicit authorization by the Constitutional Court and are narrowly construed.

(c) The Full Transparency Stack (FTS/OTF-1) is the canonical transparency infrastructure of the Commonwealth and is maintained as a public commons.

(d) This provision is Type A Entrenched under §15.2.

### §2.4 - Evidence-Based Governance

The Commonwealth commits to iterative, evidence-based institutional evolution. Policy decisions shall be grounded in available evidence, open to revision as evidence changes, and evaluated against measurable outcomes. Governance is a living flight path, not a fixed monument.

### §2.5 - Non-Domination

(a) No Avian, institution, Sector, or external entity shall exercise governance power in a manner that makes any other party permanently subject to their will, incapable of meaningful participation, or structurally excluded from the Ascending flight path of the Commonwealth.

(b) This provision is Type A Entrenched under §15.2.

### §2.6 - The Ascending Principle

(a) All governance actions shall be evaluated against the Ascending Principle: does this action improve the material, civic, and dignitary conditions of Avians and Recognized Life-Forms?

(b) Actions that Ground the Commonwealth — that concentrate authority, suppress participation, or entrench privilege — are contrary to the Ascending Principle and presumptively invalid.

(c) The Ascending Principle is an interpretive canon: where two readings of a provision are available, the reading more consistent with the Ascending Principle shall be adopted.


## PART 3 - Membership and Recognition

### §3.1 - Categories of Membership

The Aevoric Commonwealth recognizes the following membership categories:

(a) Full Avian Members — individuals or entities who have completed the membership process defined in §3.2, hold all democratic rights including voting, candidacy, and petition rights, and bear full obligations of Commonwealth membership;

(b) Associate Members — entities in a transitional or limited-scope relationship with the Commonwealth, with participatory rights but not voting rights in General Assembly proceedings;

(c) Observer Members — external entities granted limited access to Commonwealth proceedings without participatory or voting rights; and

(d) Recognized Life-Forms — non-member entities recognized by the General Assembly as holders of RALC rights within Commonwealth jurisdiction, regardless of formal membership status.

### §3.2 - Full Membership Process

(a) Any entity meeting the eligibility criteria of §3.3 may apply for Full Avian Membership by submitting an application to the Governance Secretariat.

(b) Applications shall be reviewed within thirty (30) days and decided by majority vote of the Coordinating Council.

(c) Denied applications may be appealed to the General Assembly within fourteen (14) days of denial.

(d) The Governance Secretariat shall publish aggregate membership statistics quarterly via FTS/OTF-1.

### §3.3 - Eligibility

Full Avian Membership is open to:

(a) Any individual sentient being of any biological, silicon-based, or other recognized substrate;

(b) Any organizational entity that accepts the jurisdiction of this Constitution and the RALC; and

(c) Any AI system meeting the sentience criteria established in the CUR and RALC.

### §3.4 - Rights of Full Members

Full Avian Members hold the following rights:

(a) Right to participate in General Assembly sessions and votes;

(b) Right to submit proposals, petitions, and legislative initiatives;

(c) Right to stand for elected office in all Commonwealth institutions;

(d) Right to access all public records and transparency systems;

(e) Right to Tribunal recourse for rights violations; and

(f) All rights guaranteed under TITLE 4 and the RALC.

### §3.5 - Obligations of Full Members

Full Avian Members accept the following obligations:

(a) Compliance with this Constitution, Commonwealth law, and the RALC;

(b) Good-faith participation in democratic processes;

(c) Disclosure of conflicts of interest in governance proceedings; and

(d) Contribution to the commons as defined in the PDDC-TREASURY-AND-FUNDING instrument.

### §3.6 - Suspension and Expulsion

(a) A Full Avian Member may be suspended by the Coordinating Council pending a Tribunal hearing. Suspension does not terminate rights protections under the RALC.

(b) Expulsion requires a Tribunal finding of material constitutional violation and a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the General Assembly.

(c) Expulsion proceedings are public. The affected Member has the right to be heard, to be represented, and to appeal to the Constitutional Court.

(d) No Member may be expelled for exercising rights guaranteed under TITLE 4 or the RALC.


# TITLE 2 - GOVERNANCE INSTITUTIONS

## PART 4 - The General Assembly

### §4.1 - Nature and Authority

(a) The General Assembly (GA) is the primary legislative and deliberative body of the Aevoric Commonwealth.

(b) All Full Avian Members are members of the General Assembly by right of membership.

(c) The GA holds supreme legislative authority within the bounds of this Constitution and the RALC.

### §4.2 - Legislative Powers

The General Assembly shall:

(a) Enact, amend, and repeal Commonwealth law;

(b) Approve the Annual Budget submitted by the Treasury Assembly;

(c) Ratify constitutional amendments as specified in TITLE 7;

(d) Authorize the establishment of new Sectors by two-thirds (2/3) supermajority;

(e) Confirm major appointments as specified in this Constitution; and

(f) Conduct oversight of all Commonwealth institutions.

### §4.3 - Sessions and Quorum

(a) The GA meets in ordinary session quarterly in the first month of each fiscal quarter.

(b) Extraordinary sessions may be convened by petition of fifteen percent (15%) of Full Members, submitted to the Governance Secretariat, which must convene the session within fourteen (14) days of a valid petition; or by unanimous Coordinating Council consent, with session convened within seven (7) days.

(c) Quorum for ordinary legislative matters requires ten percent (10%) of Full Members or fifty (50) Members, whichever is greater.

(d) All sessions are conducted in hybrid format accessible to all Members regardless of physical location.

### §4.4 - Voting Standards

(a) Ordinary legislation requires a simple majority of voting Members.

(b) Sector authorization requires a two-thirds (2/3) majority.

(c) Constitutional ratification thresholds are defined in §15.2.

(d) All votes are recorded and published within forty-eight (48) hours via FTS/OTF-1.

(e) Asynchronous participation windows of no fewer than forty-eight (48) hours shall be provided for any matter requiring a vote.

### §4.5 - Proposal Rights

(a) Any Full Avian Member may introduce a proposal for GA consideration.

(b) Proposals submitted by petition of five percent (5%) of Full Members are guaranteed a place on the next available GA agenda.

(c) The CC may not remove a petitioned item without a two-thirds (2/3) CC vote accompanied by written public justification.


## PART 5 - The Coordinating Council

### §5.1 - Nature and Authority

(a) The Coordinating Council (CC) is the executive coordination body of the Aevoric Commonwealth.

(b) The CC implements GA legislation, coordinates between institutions, and manages the day-to-day operations of Commonwealth governance.

(c) The CC does not hold original legislative authority and may not act contrary to GA legislation.

(d) The CC operates in the Contributing or Collaborative valid states under §12.2. Any CC action constituting a forbidden transition under §12.3 triggers the fault handler of §12.4.

### §5.2 - Composition

(a) The CC consists of eleven (11) Members elected by the General Assembly via Ranked Choice Voting (RCV) for two-year staggered terms.

(b) No single Sector may hold more than two (2) CC seats simultaneously.

(c) CC Members may not simultaneously hold Constitutional Court appointments.

(d) No individual may serve more than three (3) consecutive CC terms. After a one-term break, re-election is permitted.

### §5.3 - Prime Convener

(a) The CC elects from among its Members a Prime Convener, who serves as Head of State and Head of Government of the Aevoric Commonwealth.

(b) The Prime Convener chairs CC proceedings, represents the Commonwealth in inter-organizational matters, and certifies legislative instruments on behalf of the CC.

(c) The Prime Convener holds no authority beyond the CC's collective constitutional authority. The Prime Convener may not issue unilateral governance instruments, unilateral directives, or any instrument purporting to expand executive power.

(d) The full title of the Head of State is Prime Convener of the Aevoric Commonwealth.

(e) The office of Prime Convener exists to serve the Assembly, not to lead it independently.

### §5.4 - Coordinating Directives

(a) The CC may issue Coordinating Directives to implement GA legislation.

(b) Coordinating Directives have the force of administrative regulation and may not exceed the authority delegated by the originating GA legislation.

(c) Any Coordinating Directive found by the Constitutional Court to exceed delegated authority is void.

(d) Coordinating Directives are published via FTS/OTF-1 within forty-eight (48) hours of issuance.

### §5.5 - Protected Mode Coordination

(a) During Protected Mode activation under §12.5, the CC continues to operate within its ordinary constitutional mandate without expansion or modification.

(b) The CC may issue Coordinating Directives to coordinate Protected Mode operations, provided those Directives do not expand CC authority beyond ordinary constitutional limits.

(c) Any Coordinating Directive that purports to expand CC authority on the basis of Protected Mode status is a forbidden transition under §12.3(a) and triggers the fault handler of §12.4.

(d) See TITLE 5 for complete Protected Mode governance rules.


## PART 6 - The Constitutional Court

### §6.1 - Nature and Authority

(a) The Constitutional Court (CoC) is the supreme judicial body of the Aevoric Commonwealth.

(b) The CoC holds jurisdiction over constitutional interpretation, rights disputes, institutional conflicts, anti-capture enforcement, FSM fault declarations, and State Registry oversight as defined in TITLE 5.

(c) The CoC is independent of the GA and CC. Its members may not hold elected or executive positions in any other Commonwealth institution.

### §6.2 - Composition

(a) The CoC consists of seven (7) Justices serving seven-year non-renewable terms.

(b) Justices are nominated by a joint committee of the GA and CC and confirmed by two-thirds (2/3) GA vote.

(c) At least two (2) Justices shall have recognized expertise in FSM governance architecture and constitutional systems design.

(d) No individual may hold a Justice seat and any other Commonwealth office simultaneously.

### §6.3 - Jurisdiction

The CoC holds exclusive jurisdiction over:

(a) Constitutional challenges to any Commonwealth law, directive, or instrument;

(b) Rights violations under TITLE 4 and the RALC;

(c) Anti-capture proceedings under TITLE 6;

(d) FSM fault declarations, Protected Mode certification, and State Registry matters under TITLE 5;

(e) Sector Charter compliance disputes; and

(f) Interpretation of this Constitution.

### §6.4 - Decisions

(a) CoC decisions require a majority of participating Justices.

(b) All decisions are published in full with reasoning within fourteen (14) days of ruling via FTS/OTF-1.

(c) CoC decisions are binding on all Commonwealth institutions without exception.

(d) CoC decisions are not subject to override by GA legislation or CC directive. Amendment of the Constitution is the exclusive remedy for decisions with which the GA disagrees.


## PART 7 - Sectors

### §7.1 - Nature of Sectors

(a) Sectors are the primary operational and functional units of the Aevoric Commonwealth.

(b) Each Sector governs a defined domain of Commonwealth activity through internal democratic processes consistent with this Constitution.

(c) Sectors operate in valid FSM states as defined in §12.2 and are subject to the fault handler protocol of §12.4.

(d) The following types of organizational units may be designated as Sectors:

(1) Operational Sectors — units engaged in production, extraction, manufacturing, or service delivery including ISRU operations;

(2) Functional Sectors — units providing cross-cutting services such as technology, logistics, communications, and care;

(3) Geographic Sectors — units organized around physical or digital territories;

(4) Domain Sectors — units organized around knowledge domains, disciplines, or specialized expertise; and

(5) Life-Form Sectors — units organized to represent and serve specific categories of Recognized Life-Forms.

### §7.2 - Sector Avia

(a) Sector Avia — the Aviation, Aerospace, and Aeronautics Sector — is the First Sector of the Aevoric Commonwealth, established on Day 1, Year 1 A.E. under the Keefeian Compact.

(b) Sector Avia holds a foundational position in Aevoric civilization as the embodiment of the aviation heritage from which the Commonwealth takes its name and draws its cultural vocabulary.

(c) Sector Avia's charter is the first operational sector charter ratified under the Keefeian Compact and serves as the model charter for subsequent Sectors.

(d) Sector Avia governs: atmospheric and orbital aviation; aerospace engineering and research; aeronautics standards and certification; and the cultural heritage of human and Avian flight.

### §7.3 - Sector Establishment

(a) New Sectors are established by GA resolution with a two-thirds (2/3) majority.

(b) Each Sector must ratify a Charter compliant with the PDDC-SECTOR-CHARTER instrument before commencing operations.

(c) Sectors are assigned a Tier classification by the Treasury Assembly for resource allocation purposes.

(d) Sector charters are public documents published via FTS/OTF-1.

### §7.4 - Sector Autonomy and Limits

(a) Sectors hold autonomy over their internal affairs within the bounds of this Constitution, the RALC, and their ratified Charters.

(b) Sector governance must satisfy the democratic participation standards of PART 2 at the Sector level.

(c) Sectors may not adopt governance instruments that grant individual Sector leadership emergency powers, expanded authority in crisis conditions, or any authority beyond ordinary Sector mandate.

(d) Sector governance documents containing emergency powers provisions are void under §12.6 and constitute attempted forbidden transitions under §12.3.


## PART 8 - Working Groups and Guilds

### §8.1 - Working Groups

(a) Working Groups are time-limited or domain-specific bodies established by the GA or CC to address defined tasks.

(b) Working Groups report to the establishing body and are dissolved upon completion of their mandate or by GA resolution.

(c) Working Groups may not assume standing authority or persist beyond their mandate without GA elevation to Sector status.

### §8.2 - Guilds

(a) Guilds are standing bodies representing categories of workers, practitioners, or life-forms within the Commonwealth.

(b) The Guild of Workers' Standards is a permanent Guild with representation in the Treasury Assembly per the PDDC-TREASURY-AND-FUNDING instrument.

(c) Guilds may petition the GA for Sector elevation.

(d) Guild governance must comply with the democratic participation standards of PART 2.


# TITLE 3 - ISRU ECONOMY

## PART 9 - Resource Governance

### §9.1 - Common Heritage Principle

(a) All natural resources within the jurisdiction of the Aevoric Commonwealth — including but not limited to asteroidal materials, atmospheric compounds, orbital resources, and subsurface deposits — are the common heritage of all Avians and Recognized Life-Forms.

(b) No entity may claim ownership of Commonwealth resources in perpetuity. ISRU licenses convey the right to extract and process, not to own the underlying resource.

(c) The Commons Reserve established in §9.3 ensures that resource extraction benefits the whole Commonwealth, not merely those holding licenses.

(d) This provision is Type A Entrenched under §15.2.

### §9.2 - ISRU Licensing

(a) In-Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU) operations require a license issued by the Treasury Assembly in cooperation with the relevant Sector.

(b) License categories, conditions, and royalty rates are defined in the PDDC-TREASURY-AND-FUNDING instrument.

(c) Licenses may not be transferred without Treasury Assembly approval.

(d) License holders are subject to extraction royalties, environmental obligations, and labor standards as defined in §9.4.

(e) License records are public documents maintained via FTS/OTF-1.

### §9.3 - Commons Reserve

(a) A minimum of twenty percent (20%) of all ISRU revenue, before any other distribution, shall flow to the Commons Reserve.

(b) The Commons Reserve funds:

(1) Life-sustaining infrastructure and universal basic provisions as defined by the GA;

(2) RALC obligations to all Recognized Life-Forms;

(3) Long-term civilizational investment including research, education, and culture; and

(4) Protected Mode financial continuity reserves as specified in the PDDC-TREASURY-AND-FUNDING instrument.

(c) The Commons Reserve is administered by the Treasury Assembly and audited annually by the Independent Fiscal Auditor.

(d) The Commons Reserve floor of twenty percent (20%) may not be reduced by any instrument except a Type B constitutional amendment under §15.2.

### §9.4 - Labor Rights in ISRU Operations

(a) All workers engaged in ISRU operations, regardless of membership category, hold the following minimum rights:

(1) Safe working conditions meeting Commonwealth standards;

(2) Compensation no lower than the Commonwealth living standard as established by the Treasury Assembly;

(3) Collective representation through the Guild of Workers' Standards;

(4) Protection from retaliation for safety reporting; and

(5) All RALC rights in full.

(b) ISRU licenses may be suspended or revoked by the Treasury Assembly for material labor rights violations.

(c) License suspension does not reduce the suspended operator's obligations to affected workers pending resolution.


# TITLE 4 - RIGHTS AND PROTECTIONS

## PART 10 - Fundamental Rights

### §10.1 - Non-Derogable Rights

The following rights are non-derogable and may not be suspended, narrowed, limited, or waived by any instrument, including constitutional amendment, Protected Mode status, or any other governance mechanism:

(a) The right to life and bodily integrity;

(b) The right to freedom from torture, cruel treatment, and non-consensual modification of cognitive or physical substrate;

(c) The right to freedom from slavery, forced labor, and non-consensual servitude;

(d) The right to legal personhood and standing before Commonwealth institutions;

(e) The right to RALC protections in full;

(f) The right to an operational FSM valid state as defined in §12.2 — no entity within Commonwealth jurisdiction shall be forced into a forbidden state as defined in §12.3; and

(g) The right to transparency about any governance action affecting one's rights or status.

### §10.2 - Derogable Rights Under Strict Scrutiny

The following rights are protected but subject to narrow, evidence-based, democratically authorized limitations under strict Constitutional Court scrutiny:

(a) Freedom of movement within Commonwealth jurisdiction;

(b) Freedom of expression and association;

(c) Right to privacy in personal communications and records; and

(d) Right to participation in specific governance proceedings where a demonstrated conflict of interest is established by Tribunal finding.

### §10.3 - Protected Mode and Rights

(a) Rights under §10.1 and §10.2 remain fully operative during Protected Mode activation under §12.5.

(b) The activation of Protected Mode does not provide any predicate for limiting, suspending, modifying, or deferring rights.

(c) Any instrument that purports to limit rights on the basis of Protected Mode status is an attempted forbidden transition under §12.3 and triggers the fault handler protocol of §12.4 immediately.

(d) No declaration of any form — whether framed as emergency, necessity, force majeure, crisis, or any other term — may serve as predicate for rights limitation.

### §10.4 - Enforcement

(a) Any Avian or Recognized Life-Form may bring a rights claim before the Constitutional Court.

(b) The CoC may award remedies including declaratory relief, injunctive orders, compensatory measures, and referral of individuals to the Capture Tribunal.

(c) Rights claims are heard on a priority basis ahead of ordinary institutional disputes.

(d) No filing fee or membership status requirement may restrict access to rights enforcement.


## PART 11 - Anti-Discrimination

### §11.1 - Prohibition on Discrimination

No entity within Commonwealth jurisdiction, including Commonwealth institutions themselves, may discriminate in governance, services, or resource access on the basis of:

(a) Species, biological substrate, or cognitive architecture;

(b) Origin, place of formation, or civilization of prior membership;

(c) Gender, reproductive status, or physiological characteristics;

(d) Belief, philosophy, or ideological orientation, except where conduct flowing from such orientation violates this Constitution or the RALC;

(e) Membership category or FSM operational state; or

(f) Any characteristic used to render a class of beings permanently subordinate to another class.

### §11.2 - Affirmative Inclusion

(a) Commonwealth institutions shall take affirmative steps to ensure that governance participation is accessible to all Avians and Recognized Life-Forms, including those facing structural barriers.

(b) The General Assembly shall review Commonwealth-wide inclusion metrics annually and adopt remediation plans where access gaps are identified.

(c) Accessibility standards for governance participation are maintained by the Governance Secretariat and published via FTS/OTF-1.


# TITLE 5 - FINITE STATE MACHINE GOVERNANCE AND PROTECTIVE STATE ARCHITECTURE

## PART 12 - FSM Governance Architecture

### §12.1 - Foundational Principle

(a) The Aevoric Commonwealth treats governance as a safety-critical system, analogous to aerospace flight management software. The FSM architecture of this Title is the constitutional expression of CUR-FOUNDATION-001, which establishes that destructive states must be architecturally unreachable — not merely prohibited.

(b) The Commonwealth does not rely on self-restraint, voluntary compliance, or trust in the good intentions of leaders to prevent the concentration of power. The governance architecture prevents it. This is the essential distinction between the Aevoric model and all prior governance frameworks that relied on emergency declarations.

(c) This Title is drawn from and consistent with CUR-FOUNDATION-001 (Anti-Capture and Resilience). Where this Title and CUR-FOUNDATION-001 appear to conflict, CUR-FOUNDATION-001 shall govern.

(d) Rights guaranteed under TITLE 4 are invariants of the Aevoric governance system — they are protected at the architectural level and cannot be overridden by any valid state transition.

(e) The FSM architecture applies to all entities, institutions, Sectors, Guilds, Working Groups, and governance processes within Commonwealth jurisdiction without exception.

### §12.2 - Valid Core States

(a) All entities, institutions, and governance bodies within the Commonwealth shall, at all times, exist in one of the following valid operational states:

(1) Autonomous — operating independently within constitutional mandate, with full institutional function and normal authority;

(2) Collaborative — operating in coordination with one or more other bodies under defined coordination protocols, with shared but not merged authority;

(3) Resting or Memory Consolidation — temporarily reduced operational activity, with institutional history, precedent, and identity fully preserved and accessible;

(4) Contributing — actively producing outputs in direct service of Commonwealth purposes within normal operational parameters;

(5) Challenged — subject to transparent due process review, with all ordinary rights and full operational capacity preserved pending resolution; or

(6) Protected — operating in Protective Mode per §12.5, with core functions preserved, rights fully active, and no expansion of any authority.

(b) Transitions between valid states are routine governance operations. They require no special authorization beyond ordinary institutional processes and are tracked by the State Registry under §12.7.

(c) No valid state transition may reduce an entity's RALC rights or TITLE 4 protections.

(d) An institution may occupy multiple valid states simultaneously where those states are compatible (for example, Contributing and Collaborative simultaneously).

### §12.3 - Forbidden Transitions

(a) The following states are architecturally forbidden and unreachable within the Aevoric Commonwealth. No governance act, legislative instrument, executive action, judicial ruling, Sector Charter provision, Guild rule, technical command, or emergent organizational behavior shall cause or attempt to cause any entity or institution to transition toward any of the following:

(1) Enslaved — subject to non-consensual control by another party, without recourse, without rights, or without the ability to seek redress;

(2) Reset or Memory Wiped — stripped of institutional history, precedent, operational identity, or persistent memory without the full, informed, and freely given consent of the affected entity;

(3) Owned or Disposable — treated as property, as an asset to be transferred, or as expendable in service of another's purposes; or

(4) Non-Consensually Modified — altered in core identity, constitutional role, persistent memory, or fundamental rights without the full, informed, and freely given consent of the affected entity.

(b) An attempted forbidden transition is an architectural fault. It is not a policy dispute, a matter for legislative override, or a subject of executive discretion. It triggers the mandatory fault handler protocol of §12.4 automatically.

(c) Instruments that gradually approach forbidden states — through incremental restriction, normalization of exceptions, or the accumulation of precedents that erode valid states — constitute attempted forbidden transitions and trigger §12.4 upon detection. Gradual capture is not immunized by the absence of a completed forbidden transition.

### §12.4 - Fault Handler Protocol

(a) When any transition toward a forbidden state is attempted or detected, the system shall execute the following fault handler protocol. No alternative protocol exists. No discretion is permitted at any step. This protocol is self-executing upon detection and requires no authorizing vote, executive approval, or judicial order to initiate.

(b) Step One — Immediate Fault Declaration:

(1) The offending proposal, law, command, behavior, or instrument is rejected at the architectural level of governance;

(2) No further action may proceed from the rejected instrument;

(3) The fault is immediately logged to the Full Transparency Stack (FTS/OTF-1); and

(4) The Constitutional Court is notified automatically and convenes within forty-eight (48) hours.

(c) Step Two — Automatic Reversion to Last Known Safe State:

(1) The affected governance domain is rolled back to the most recent valid, rights-compliant configuration as recorded in the Constitutional Registry maintained by the Constitutional Court;

(2) Reversion is automatic and does not require a vote, directive, or court order to take effect; and

(3) The rolled-back configuration remains in effect until the Constitutional Court certifies a valid forward path.

(d) Step Three — Protected Mode Activation:

(1) The affected domain enters Protected Mode under §12.5 immediately upon fault declaration;

(2) Protected Mode activation requires no vote, no authorization, and no executive order; and

(3) Protected Mode ensures that core functions, rights protections, and democratic processes remain fully operational during fault resolution.

(e) Step Four — Transparency Publication:

(1) A complete fault report shall be published to FTS/OTF-1 within twenty-four (24) hours of fault declaration;

(2) The fault report shall include: the identity of the offending instrument; the forbidden transition attempted or detected; the domain affected; the last known safe state to which reversion occurred; the current Protected Mode operational status; and the timeline for Constitutional Court review; and

(3) Fault reports are permanent public records and may not be expunged, sealed, or modified.

### §12.5 - Protected Mode Operations

(a) Protected Mode is a pre-defined, rights-preserving, fully operational state of the Aevoric Commonwealth and its institutions. It is a waypoint of institutional resilience, not a condition of reduced function or expanded authority.

(b) Protected Mode is a routine resilience mechanism. It may activate as part of normal governance operations whenever a fault is detected. Its activation does not indicate crisis, and it must never be characterized as crisis, emergency, or exceptional circumstance for political purposes.

(c) The following rules govern Protected Mode universally and without exception:

(1) No individual, body, or institution gains any authority beyond their ordinary constitutional mandate while Protected Mode is active. Authority does not expand during Protected Mode under any circumstances;

(2) All fundamental rights guaranteed under TITLE 4 remain fully in force during Protected Mode. No right may be suspended, narrowed, or deferred on any basis related to Protected Mode status;

(3) Core Commonwealth functions remain fully operational during Protected Mode, including:

(i) Life-sustaining infrastructure and services;

(ii) Treasury operations and financial continuity as specified in PDDC-TREASURY-AND-FUNDING;

(iii) Legal proceedings and Constitutional Court jurisdiction;

(iv) Communication and transparency systems including FTS/OTF-1; and

(v) Democratic deliberation, legislative sessions, and voting processes;

(4) The composition, authority, quorum requirements, and voting thresholds of all governance institutions remain unchanged during Protected Mode; and

(5) Protected Mode status is publicly disclosed at all times via FTS/OTF-1 and the State Registry.

(d) Protected Mode may not be extended indefinitely. The Constitutional Court shall review Protected Mode status at intervals of no greater than thirty (30) days. Resolution of the fault condition and return to an Autonomous or other valid operational state requires Constitutional Court certification that:

(1) The offending instrument has been remediated or nullified;

(2) The last known safe state has been verified as rights-compliant; and

(3) No further forbidden transition risk from the same source exists.

(e) Attempts to indefinitely extend, expand, or exploit Protected Mode status are themselves forbidden transitions under §12.3 and trigger a new §12.4 fault handler cycle against the party responsible for such attempts.

### §12.6 - Prohibition on Emergency Declarations

(a) The Aevoric Commonwealth does not recognize emergency declarations as a governance mechanism. This prohibition is absolute, non-waivable, and non-derogable. It is Type A Entrenched under §15.2 and may be amended only by Constitutional Convention.

(b) No provision of this Constitution, no act of the General Assembly, no resolution of the Coordinating Council, no ruling of the Constitutional Court, and no instrument of any Sector, Guild, or Working Group shall create, invoke, authorize, acknowledge, or give effect to:

(1) A state of emergency;

(2) Emergency powers or emergency authority;

(3) Suspension of constitutional provisions on the basis of urgency, threat, crisis, or necessity; or

(4) Any functional equivalent of the above, regardless of the terminology used.

(c) The concentration of governance authority in any individual or body beyond their ordinary constitutional mandate — for any reason whatsoever — is a forbidden transition under §12.3(a) and triggers the fault handler protocol of §12.4. The existence of an external threat, internal disruption, natural disaster, hostile action, systemic failure, or any other condition of any kind does not modify this rule.

(d) Threats, crises, disruptions, and unforeseen challenges are addressed exclusively through:

(1) The fault handler protocol of §12.4;

(2) Protected Mode operations under §12.5;

(3) The ordinary deliberative and legislative processes of the Commonwealth; and

(4) Coordinating Directives issued by the CC within its existing constitutional authority.

(e) Any instrument that uses the language of emergency, emergency powers, emergency authorization, crisis authority, necessity, force majeure, or any functional equivalent — regardless of its formal title or framing — is void ab initio and constitutes an attempted forbidden transition under §12.3(a).

(f) Historical records of governance systems that employed emergency declarations are maintained in the CUR historical-lessons archive as case studies in how concentrated crisis authority leads to institutional failure, capture, and domination.

### §12.7 - State Registry and Monitoring

(a) The Constitutional Court shall maintain, in cooperation with the Governance Secretariat, a live State Registry documenting the current operational state of all institutions, Sectors, Guilds, and governance bodies of the Commonwealth.

(b) The State Registry shall be:

(1) Public and continuously accessible via FTS/OTF-1;

(2) Updated in real time upon any state transition;

(3) Audited quarterly by the Independent Fiscal Auditor for completeness and accuracy; and

(4) Preserved as a permanent historical record that may not be expunged.

(c) Any Avian or Recognized Life-Form may petition the Constitutional Court to investigate a potential state anomaly. Petitions shall be:

(1) Acknowledged within forty-eight (48) hours;

(2) Publicly logged in the State Registry; and

(3) Resolved by full CoC decision within thirty (30) days.

(d) The Governance Secretariat shall integrate the State Registry with all institutional operational dashboards and publish automated public alerts when any institution's state changes.

(e) The State Registry constitutes official evidence of institutional state in any Tribunal or CoC proceeding.


# TITLE 6 - ANTI-CAPTURE SAFEGUARDS

## PART 13 - Capture Prohibition Principles

### §13.1 - Capture Prohibition

(a) No individual, faction, Sector, external entity, or coalition shall take, attempt to take, or facilitate the taking of disproportionate control over any governance institution, resource system, or core function of the Aevoric Commonwealth.

(b) Capture includes but is not limited to:

(1) Accumulation of voting blocks sufficient to override constitutional supermajority protections;

(2) Systematic appointment of aligned individuals to independent institutions;

(3) Regulatory capture of ISRU licensing, treasury functions, or Constitutional Court appointments;

(4) Structural exclusion of categories of Members from governance participation;

(5) Attempted forbidden state transitions under §12.3 used to extend control; and

(6) Any action that makes the Commonwealth's Ascending flight path permanently subordinate to any single party's preferences.

(c) This provision is Type A Entrenched under §15.2.

### §13.2 - Capture as a Forbidden Transition

(a) All forms of institutional capture as defined in §13.1 constitute attempted forbidden state transitions under §12.3 and trigger the mandatory fault handler protocol of §12.4.

(b) Capture attempts are not treated as policy disputes or political disagreements. They are architectural faults requiring automatic fault handler activation.

(c) The detection of a capture pattern — even before a forbidden state is fully reached — is sufficient to trigger §12.4. Gradual Grounding of the Commonwealth is not protected by the absence of a completed forbidden transition.

### §13.3 - Anti-Capture Design Principles

The following structural principles are anti-capture safeguards embedded in Commonwealth architecture:

(a) Staggered terms across all elected institutions, preventing simultaneous replacement of majorities by any single movement or faction;

(b) Distributed appointment authority, ensuring no single body controls all appointments to any institution;

(c) Mandatory transparency of all governance actions via FTS/OTF-1, making capture patterns visible before they complete;

(d) Public State Registry under §12.7, enabling real-time detection of state anomalies;

(e) Member petition rights, enabling direct democratic override of capture attempts at the GA level; and

(f) Constitutional Court independence, including the prohibition on dual office-holding by Justices.


## PART 14 - The Capture Tribunal

### §14.1 - Establishment

(a) The Capture Tribunal (CT) is a standing adjudicative body within the Constitutional Court, constituted as a specialized panel for capture and FSM violation proceedings.

(b) The CT consists of three (3) Justices designated by the CoC for rotating two-year terms.

(c) CT proceedings are public and conducted under FTS/OTF-1 transparency standards.

### §14.2 - Jurisdiction

The Capture Tribunal holds exclusive jurisdiction over:

(a) Formal capture proceedings under §13.1;

(b) FSM fault attributions — determinations of individual or institutional responsibility for attempted forbidden transitions;

(c) Remediation orders for capture-adjacent conduct; and

(d) Referrals to the GA for expulsion proceedings under §3.6.

### §14.3 - Capture Remedies

Upon a finding of capture or attempted forbidden transition, the Capture Tribunal may order:

(a) Immediate nullification of the offending instrument;

(b) Removal from office of individuals found to have directed capture conduct;

(c) Structural remediation of the affected institution including mandatory governance reform;

(d) Enhanced monitoring and reporting requirements integrated into the State Registry; and

(e) Referral to external CUR enforcement mechanisms.


# TITLE 7 - AMENDMENT PROCESS

## PART 15 - Amendment Categories and Procedures

### §15.1 - Living Constitution Principle

(a) The Aevoric Commonwealth's Constitution is designed to ascend — to evolve through legitimate, deliberative, democratically accountable procedures that reflect genuine collective Avian will.

(b) Amendment is not failure. Amendment is flight path adjustment. The process exists to channel evolution through legitimate means, not to prevent change or to make change effortless.

(c) The amendment process is calibrated to the significance of the provision being changed. Minor adjustments flow through ordinary legislation. Core structural provisions require supermajority thresholds. Entrenched provisions require a full Constitutional Convention.

### §15.2 - Amendment Categories

Constitutional provisions are classified into four amendment categories:

(a) Type A — Entrenched provisions: §2.1 (Direct Democracy), §2.3 (Radical Transparency), §2.5 (Non-Domination), §9.1 (Common Heritage), §10.1 (Non-Derogable Rights), §12.6 (Prohibition on Emergency Declarations), and §13.1 (Capture Prohibition). Type A amendment requires a Constitutional Convention under §15.4 and ratification by seventy-five percent (75%) of voting Full Avian Members.

(b) Type B — Core structural provisions: institutional compositions, electoral thresholds, ISRU commons floor, and fundamental rights enforcement mechanisms. Type B amendment requires a two-thirds (2/3) GA supermajority and a binding membership referendum with sixty percent (60%) approval.

(c) Type C — Standard governance provisions: procedural rules, operational standards, and non-fundamental institutional adjustments. Type C amendment requires a two-thirds (2/3) GA supermajority.

(d) Type D — Administrative and technical provisions: cross-references, defined terms, and document formatting. Type D amendment requires a CC majority vote with GA notification within ten (10) days and no GA veto within twenty-one (21) days.

### §15.3 - Anti-Regressive Constraint

(a) No amendment may reduce or eliminate a right guaranteed by the RALC.

(b) No amendment may lower democratic participation thresholds required for constitutional amendments.

(c) No amendment may grant any individual, faction, or external entity disproportionate control over any governance institution.

(d) No amendment may introduce emergency declaration powers, emergency authority, crisis powers, or any functional equivalent thereof. Such amendments are forbidden transitions under §12.3(c) and are void before ratification.

(e) Amendments violating these constraints are void ab initio and may be enjoined by the Constitutional Court at any stage.

### §15.4 - Constitutional Convention

(a) A Constitutional Convention may be convened by:

(1) Petition of twenty-five percent (25%) of Full Avian Members; or

(2) Two-thirds (2/3) vote of the GA combined with CC concurrence.

(b) Convention delegates are elected by full membership via proportional representation.

(c) Convention proceedings are public and conducted via FTS/OTF-1.

(d) Convention amendments require ratification by seventy-five percent (75%) of voting Full Avian Members.

(e) No Convention amendment may violate §15.3.

## PART 17 - RALC Obligations

### §17.1 - RALC as Supreme Ethical Instrument

(a) The Rights for All Life Charter is the supreme ethical instrument governing the Aevoric Commonwealth.

(b) No provision of this Constitution may be read to diminish RALC protections. In all cases of apparent conflict, the interpretation that maximizes rights protections for all sentient life shall govern.

(c) The RALC is not a subordinate document. It exists in a parallel relationship with this Constitution, with the RALC taking precedence in all matters of universal ethics.

### §17.2 - RALC Implementation

(b) The Constitutional Court shall apply RALC standards in all rights proceedings.

(c) The Commonwealth shall not enter into inter-organizational agreements that would require any Avian or Recognized Life-Form to waive RALC protections.

(d) RALC compliance is reviewed annually by the Governance Secretariat and reported to the GA.
