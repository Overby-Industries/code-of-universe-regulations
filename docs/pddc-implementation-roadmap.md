# TITLE 0 - PDDC Implementation Roadmap — Flight Path for Institutional Buildout

## PART 0 - The Aevoric Commonwealth (Aevoria)

### §0.1 - Coordinating Council — Issued under PDDC §18.3 Effective: 

Day 1, Year 1 Aevoric Era — 20 May 2026 C.E.

(a) Status: Binding — Living Document — Updated per §1.2

### §0.2 - Preamble

This Implementation Roadmap is a living operational document issued under authority of the Coordinating Council in accordance with PDDC §18.3. It translates the constitutional commitments of the Progressive Direct Democracy Constitution into phased, accountable, time-bound institutional actions. It is not aspirational. It is binding on all transitional and incoming governance bodies.

A constitution is a promise made to the future. This Roadmap is how the Aevoric Commonwealth keeps it. Each Phase is a waypoint on the Ascending flight path from the Keefeian Founding to a fully realized democratic civilization. Failure to clear a waypoint is not a crisis — it triggers the review process of §1.4 and a mandatory remediation plan. The flight continues.

This Roadmap is organized into five Phases spanning a thirty-six month buildout period beginning Day 1, Year 1 A.E. (20 May 2026 C.E.), followed by steady-state governance in Phase 5. Each Phase specifies objectives, waypoint milestones, responsible actors, success metrics, and continuity protocols. Failure to meet Phase waypoints triggers automatic review by the Constitutional Court and a mandatory remediation plan submitted to the General Assembly.

The FSM governance architecture of TITLE 5 of the Constitution applies to this Roadmap as a governing instrument. Any Roadmap amendment that would constitute a forbidden transition under §12.3 of the Constitution is void.

Per Avia, Ad Astra.


# TITLE 1 - ROADMAP GOVERNANCE AND FOUNDATIONS

## PART 1 - Roadmap Authority and Administration

### §1.1 - Adoption and Amendment Authority

(a) This Roadmap is adopted by the Transitional Coordinating Council and ratified by the initial General Assembly.

(b) Amendments to Phase structure require a two-thirds (2/3) CC vote and GA notification within ten (10) days.

(c) Amendments to waypoint milestone dates require a CC majority vote.

(d) Amendments to core commitments — including rights timelines, ISRU moratorium conditions, RALC integration schedule, and FSM deployment milestones — require GA supermajority.

(e) No amendment may reduce or remove FSM deployment milestones, State Registry publication milestones, or Protected Mode operational testing requirements.

### §1.2 - Implementation Dashboard

The Governance Secretariat shall maintain a public-facing Implementation Dashboard updated no less than bi-monthly showing:

(a) Current Phase and elapsed time;

(b) Waypoint milestone completion status using Green, Yellow, and Red designations;

(c) Responsible actor for each open milestone;

(d) Remediation plans for any Red-status milestones;

(e) Current FSM State Registry status for all institutions; and

(f) Link to the full audit log via FTS/OTF-1.

### §1.3 - Independent Review

(a) At the close of each Phase, an Independent Implementation Review (IIR) shall be conducted by an external assessor approved by the Constitutional Court.

(b) IIR reports are published publicly within thirty (30) days of Phase completion via FTS/OTF-1.

(c) IIR assessors may not be current CC or GA Members and must have no financial relationship with any licensed ISRU operator.

### §1.4 - Civic Participation in Roadmap Execution

(a) Members are encouraged to participate in Roadmap Working Groups established for each Phase.

(b) At least one Roadmap session per quarter shall be open to all Members in a town-hall format.

(c) Implementation decisions made without available civic input shall be flagged and documented in the Dashboard.

(d) Working Group recommendations that reach consensus carry advisory weight equivalent to a CC proposal.

### §1.5 - Waypoint Failure Protocol

(a) Any milestone designated Red-status for more than thirty (30) days triggers automatic Constitutional Court notification.

(b) The Constitutional Court shall issue a remediation order within fifteen (15) days of notification.

(c) Remediation orders are binding on the responsible actor.

(d) Phase advancement requires Constitutional Court certification that all prior Phase milestones have been completed or formally remediated.

(e) Waypoint failure is not a protected Mode trigger unless the failure itself constitutes a forbidden transition under §12.3 of the Constitution.

# TITLE 3 - CONTINUITY AND RESILIENCE

## PART 8 - Protected Mode Financial Continuity

### §8.1 - Continuity Philosophy

(a) The Aevoric Commonwealth does not have emergency powers and does not have emergency financial protocols.

(b) The FSM architecture of PDDC TITLE 5 is the exclusive mechanism for managing governance disruptions. When the system encounters a fault, it enters Protected Mode — a fully operational, rights-preserving state that requires no special authorization and grants no expanded powers.

(c) Continuity is achieved through pre-positioning, not through power concentration.

### §8.2 - Protected Mode Budget Continuity

(a) In the event Protected Mode is activated under PDDC §12.5, the most recent adopted Annual Budget remains in full force without modification.

(b) No budget allocation may be redirected, suspended, or expanded on the basis of Protected Mode status.

(c) Treasury Assembly authority does not expand during Protected Mode. The Treasury Assembly continues to perform its ordinary functions within its ordinary constitutional mandate.

(d) The Commons Reserve shall maintain a Protected Mode Continuity Reserve equal to ninety (90) days of Commons Reserve disbursements, held separately from operating funds and accessible only for life-sustaining expenditures during Protected Mode.

### §8.3 - Institutional Continuity

(a) All Roadmap Working Groups and implementation bodies continue to operate normally during Protected Mode.

(b) Roadmap milestone deadlines are tolled during Protected Mode periods, with the toll period added to the deadline upon Protected Mode resolution.

(c) The Dashboard is updated to reflect Protected Mode status and milestone tolling in real time.

### §8.4 - Prohibition on Continuity Pretext

(a) Protected Mode continuity provisions may not be used as a pretext for altering Roadmap milestones, budget allocations, institutional composition, or governance authority.

(b) Any instrument that invokes continuity, stability, resilience, or analogous terms to expand governance authority or modify rights is a forbidden transition under PDDC §12.3(a) and triggers the fault handler of §12.4.


## PART 9 - Milestone Remediation

### §9.1 - Remediation Trigger

(a) A milestone is Red-status when it is more than fourteen (14) days past its due date without completion.

(b) Red-status designation triggers automatic notification to the Constitutional Court and public disclosure via the Dashboard.

### §9.2 - Remediation Plan

(a) The responsible actor for any Red-status milestone must submit a remediation plan to the CC within seven (7) days of Red-status designation.

(b) Remediation plans must specify: the cause of non-completion; the revised completion timeline; the actions to be taken; and the resources required.

(c) Remediation plans are published via FTS/OTF-1.

### §9.3 - Constitutional Court Oversight

(a) The Constitutional Court may issue binding remediation orders for milestones that remain Red-status for thirty (30) days or more.

(b) Remediation orders may specify completion methods, transfer of responsibility, or resource allocation adjustments within existing budget authority.

(c) Failure to comply with a remediation order constitutes a constitutional violation and triggers Capture Tribunal jurisdiction.

### §9.4 - Phase Advancement

(a) Phase advancement requires Constitutional Court certification that all milestones in the prior Phase are either Green-status or formally remediated.

(b) Formal remediation means completion of all required actions under a Court-approved remediation plan, even if the original timeline was not met.

(c) Phase advancement may not be waived or accelerated by CC or GA action alone.
