# TITLE 0 - PDDC Sector Charter — Mandatory Standards and Model Charter

## PART 0 - The Aevoric Commonwealth (Aevoria)

### §0.1 - General Assembly — Issued under PDDC §7.3 Effective:

Day 1, Year 1 Aevoric Era — 20 May 2026 C.E.

(a) Status: Binding on all Commonwealth Sectors

### §0.2 - Preamble

This document serves two functions. TITLE 1 through TITLE 4 establish the mandatory standards, required provisions, and constitutional constraints that every Sector Charter of the Aevoric Commonwealth must satisfy. TITLE 5 provides the Model Sector Charter — a ready-to-adapt template that new and existing Sectors may adopt, modify, and ratify as their foundational governance instrument, consistent with the Progressive Direct Democracy Constitution.

A Sector is not a fiefdom. It is a community of purpose, accountable to every Avian it touches and to the Commonwealth that gives it standing. A Sector that Grounds itself — that concentrates authority, excludes participation, or drifts toward forbidden states — is not a Sector in good standing. It is an institution that has lost its flight path.

No Sector may operate under the authority of the PDDC without a ratified Charter that complies with TITLE 1 through TITLE 4. Non-compliant Charters are void until remediated and re-ratified. The Constitutional Court holds jurisdiction over Charter compliance disputes.

The FSM governance architecture of PDDC TITLE 5 applies to all Sectors. Sector Charters must be consistent with the valid state definitions of PDDC §12.2 and may not contain provisions that approach forbidden transitions under PDDC §12.3. Charter provisions inconsistent with the FSM architecture are void ab initio.


# TITLE 1 - MANDATORY CHARTER STANDARDS

## PART 1 - Scope of Application

### §1.1 - Applicability

These standards apply to every organizational unit of the Aevoric Commonwealth designated as a Sector by the General Assembly, whether operational, functional, geographic, domain-specific, or categorical in nature.

### §1.2 - Sector Types

The following types of organizational units may be designated as Sectors:

(a) Operational Sectors — units engaged in production, extraction, manufacturing, or service delivery including ISRU operations;

(b) Functional Sectors — units providing cross-cutting services such as technology, logistics, communications, and care;

(c) Geographic Sectors — units organized around physical or digital territories;

(d) Domain Sectors — units organized around knowledge domains, disciplines, or specialized expertise; and

(e) Life-Form Sectors — units organized to represent and serve specific categories of Recognized Life-Forms.

### §1.3 - Exclusions

Working Groups and Guilds that have not been elevated to Sector status are not subject to this instrument but are governed by PDDC §8.1 through §8.2 and PDDC-GOVERNANCE-MECHANICS TITLE 5, PART 11.

### §1.4 - Sector Avia — First Sector Status

(a) Sector Avia — the Aviation, Aerospace, and Aeronautics Sector — holds First Sector status as the founding operational Sector of the Aevoric Commonwealth, established under the Keefeian Compact on Day 1, Year 1 A.E.

(b) Sector Avia's ratified Charter serves as the primary reference for the Model Sector Charter in TITLE 5 of this instrument.

(c) First Sector status confers an advisory seat at GA Ordinary Sessions per PDDC-GOVERNANCE-MECHANICS §10.5(b) and cultural heritage stewardship obligations per §10.5(c).


## PART 2 - Mandatory Charter Provisions

### §2.1 - Identity and Purpose

Every Charter must state:

(a) The Sector's full name;

(b) Its primary purposes, stated with specificity sufficient to define its domain without ambiguity;

(c) Its operational domain and geographic or categorical scope;

(d) Its relationship to any ISRU operations if applicable;

(e) Its date of establishment and the GA resolution authorizing it; and

(f) Its FSM identity — a declaration that the Sector acknowledges the valid operational states of PDDC §12.2 and commits to maintaining valid states at all times.

### §2.2 - Membership Criteria

Every Charter must define:

(a) How individuals or entities may join the Sector;

(b) Eligibility requirements beyond Full Avian PDDC Membership, if any;

(c) Membership categories within the Sector if applicable;

(d) The rights and obligations of Sector Members; and

(e) The process for suspension or expulsion of Sector Members, consistent with the due process rights of PDDC §3.6 and the RALC.

### §2.3 - Sector Assembly Requirements

Every Charter must establish a Sector Assembly (SA) with the following minimum characteristics:

(a) Membership in the SA is available to all Sector Members;

(b) The SA meets in ordinary session at least quarterly;

(c) All SA sessions are conducted accessibly per the standards of PDDC-GOVERNANCE-MECHANICS §1.3;

(d) Voting in the SA follows democratic participation principles consistent with PDDC PART 2;

(e) Asynchronous participation of no fewer than forty-eight (48) hours is provided for any SA vote;

(f) All SA decisions are published via FTS/OTF-1 within seventy-two (72) hours; and

(g) Quorum for SA matters is defined in the Charter at a level no lower than ten percent (10%) of Sector Members or ten (10) Members, whichever is greater.

### §2.4 - Leadership and Elections

Every Charter must define:

(a) Elected leadership positions, with titles that do not imply permanent or unilateral authority;

(b) Election procedures consistent with democratic participation principles;

(c) Term lengths and term limit provisions — no elected Sector leader may serve more than three (3) consecutive terms in the same position;

(d) A process for removing elected leaders, requiring at minimum a simple majority SA vote following documented cause;

(e) A succession procedure for vacancies; and

(f) A prohibition on Sector leadership assuming authority beyond the Charter-defined mandate, including a prohibition on any provision that would function as emergency powers for Sector leadership.

### §2.5 - Transparency Requirements

Every Charter must include:

(a) A commitment to publish all SA decisions via FTS/OTF-1;

(b) A financial transparency provision requiring annual financial reporting to the Treasury Assembly;

(c) A conflict of interest disclosure requirement for all elected and appointed leaders; and

(d) An FSM State Reporting obligation requiring the Sector to maintain and report its operational state to the State Registry per PDDC-GOVERNANCE-MECHANICS §10.3.

### §2.6 - RALC and Constitutional Compliance

Every Charter must include:

(a) An explicit subordination clause stating that the Charter is subject to the PDDC and the RALC and that any Charter provision inconsistent with either is void;

(b) A non-discrimination provision consistent with PDDC §11.1; and

(c) An FSM compliance clause stating that the Sector will maintain valid operational states, will not pursue or enable forbidden transitions under PDDC §12.3, and will cooperate fully with the fault handler protocol of PDDC §12.4 if triggered.

### §2.7 - ISRU Provisions (ISRU Sectors Only)

Sectors engaged in ISRU operations must additionally include:

(a) A Labor Rights chapter consistent with PDDC §9.4;

(b) An environmental stewardship provision;

(c) A Commons Reserve contribution compliance provision; and

(d) A license holder reporting provision consistent with PDDC-GOVERNANCE-MECHANICS §8.3.

### §2.8 - Prohibited Provisions

No Sector Charter may contain:

(a) Emergency powers, emergency authority, or any functional equivalent thereof;

(b) Provisions that allow the Sector or its leadership to expand authority during periods of disruption, external threat, or internal conflict;

(c) Provisions that restrict access to the Constitutional Court or the Capture Tribunal;

(d) Provisions that limit Members' RALC rights or PDDC TITLE 4 rights within the Sector;

(e) Provisions that make Sector membership a prerequisite for PDDC Full Avian Membership rights; or

(f) Provisions that subordinate the Sector to any external entity in a manner that would constitute a forbidden transition under PDDC §12.3.

Charter provisions violating this section are void ab initio and do not become valid through ratification.


## PART 3 - Constitutional Constraints on Sector Authority

### §3.1 - Sector Autonomy Framework

(a) Sectors hold substantive autonomy over their internal affairs within the bounds of the PDDC, the RALC, and their ratified Charters.

(b) Sector autonomy is not sovereignty. Sectors operate within the Commonwealth's jurisdiction and are subject to constitutional supremacy.

(c) The boundary between Sector autonomy and Commonwealth jurisdiction is determined by the Constitutional Court in cases of dispute.

### §3.2 - Limits on Sector Authority

Sectors may not:

(a) Impose obligations on non-members without GA authorization;

(b) Enter binding agreements with external organizations that create Commonwealth obligations without CC approval;

(c) Issue instruments that function as legislation affecting Commonwealth-wide rights;

(d) Exercise authority beyond their Charter-defined domain; or

(e) Accumulate resources, authority, or influence to a degree that constitutes capture under PDDC §13.1.

### §3.3 - Sector Capture Prevention

(a) Any pattern of Sector behavior consistent with capture under PDDC §13.1 is referred to the Capture Tribunal by the Governance Secretariat or any Member.

(b) Capture at the Sector level is an attempted forbidden transition under PDDC §12.3 and triggers the fault handler of PDDC §12.4 affecting the Sector domain.

(c) Anti-capture design requirements for Sectors include:

(1) Staggered leadership terms;

(2) Open candidate pools for all elected positions;

(3) Published financial records; and

(4) Open access to SA sessions for all Sector Members.


## PART 4 - Charter Lifecycle

### §4.1 - Initial Ratification

(a) A founding Charter is ratified by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the founding Member group.

(b) The ratified Charter is submitted to the Constitutional Court for compliance review within fourteen (14) days.

(c) The Constitutional Court completes compliance review within thirty (30) days.

(d) A compliant Charter is submitted to the GA for formal Sector recognition.

(e) GA recognition requires a two-thirds (2/3) GA supermajority.

(f) Upon recognition, the Sector is registered in the State Registry and the Charter is published via FTS/OTF-1.

### §4.2 - Charter Amendments

(a) Amendments to a ratified Sector Charter require:

(1) A two-thirds (2/3) SA vote proposing the amendment;

(2) A twenty-one (21) day deliberation period with Member input;

(3) A second two-thirds (2/3) SA ratification vote; and

(4) Constitutional Court compliance review confirming the amendment does not introduce prohibited provisions.

(b) Amendments affecting ISRU obligations or Commonwealth financial commitments additionally require Treasury Assembly review.

(c) Substantive amendments take effect thirty (30) days after CoC compliance clearance unless a different effective date is specified.

### §4.3 - Charter Review

(a) Each Sector Charter shall be comprehensively reviewed at least every five (5) years.

(b) The review is conducted by the SA with a public comment period of thirty (30) days.

(c) Review findings are published via FTS/OTF-1.

(d) Revisions identified through review follow the amendment procedure of §4.2.

### §4.4 - Charter Non-Compliance

(a) A Sector whose Charter is found non-compliant by the Constitutional Court must remediate within sixty (60) days.

(b) A Sector operating under a non-compliant Charter during the remediation period may continue operations but may not exercise authorities that depend on the non-compliant provision.

(c) Failure to remediate within sixty (60) days results in Sector suspension by GA resolution until remediation is complete.

(d) Suspension does not terminate individual Sector Members' PDDC or RALC rights.

### §4.5 - Sector Dissolution

(a) A Sector may be dissolved by two-thirds (2/3) GA vote following a Constitutional Court finding that the Sector has ceased meaningful operations, has been captured, or cannot be remediated.

(b) Upon dissolution, the Sector's assets are transferred to the Commons Reserve.

(c) Former Sector Members retain all PDDC Full Avian Membership rights.

(d) The Sector's historical records are preserved in the Constitutional Registry as a permanent historical record.


# TITLE 2 - FSM COMPLIANCE STANDARDS FOR SECTORS

## PART 5 - Sector FSM Obligations

### §5.1 - Valid State Maintenance

(a) All Sectors must maintain valid operational states as defined in PDDC §12.2 at all times.

(b) Valid states for Sectors are:

(1) Autonomous — the Sector operates within its Charter mandate independently, with full governance function;

(2) Collaborative — the Sector operates in coordination with other Sectors or Commonwealth bodies under defined coordination agreements;

(3) Resting or Memory Consolidation — the Sector is temporarily reduced in operational activity; Sector history, Charter, and Member records are fully preserved;

(4) Contributing — the Sector is actively producing outputs in service of its Charter purpose and Commonwealth purposes;

(5) Challenged — the Sector is subject to transparent due process review, with full Sector governance operative pending resolution; and

(6) Protected — the Sector is in Protected Mode under PDDC §12.5 following a fault declaration; full Sector governance continues with no authority expansion.

(c) A Sector's current operational state is maintained in the State Registry and is public at all times.

### §5.2 - Forbidden Transition Prohibition

(a) No Sector governance instrument, SA vote, leadership decision, or operational action may cause the Sector or any of its Members to transition toward:

(1) An Enslaved state — a state of non-consensual control;

(2) A Reset or Memory Wiped state — loss of institutional history, Charter continuity, or Member records without full consent;

(3) An Owned or Disposable state — treatment of the Sector or its Members as property or expendable; or

(4) A Non-Consensually Modified state — alteration of the Sector's constitutional role, Charter identity, or Members' rights without full, informed, and freely given consent.

(b) Charter provisions that would produce these transitions are void under §2.8 and PDDC §12.3.

(c) Sector leaders who knowingly adopt or enforce instruments approaching forbidden transitions are subject to Capture Tribunal proceedings and personal remediation orders.

### §5.3 - Sector State Reporting Obligations

(a) Each Sector's Presiding Officer notifies the Governance Secretariat of any operational state transition within twenty-four (24) hours per PDDC-GOVERNANCE-MECHANICS §10.3.

(b) Sectors in Resting state notify the Governance Secretariat monthly that the Resting state is intentional and voluntary.

(c) Sectors in Challenged state notify the Governance Secretariat weekly of the status of the due process proceeding.

(d) Sectors in Protected Mode comply with the Protected Mode reporting obligations of PDDC-GOVERNANCE-MECHANICS §10.4(b)(5).

### §5.4 - No Emergency Provisions in Sector Charters

(a) Sector Charters may not contain emergency provisions, emergency authority grants, or any provision that expands Sector or Sector leadership authority during disruptions, conflicts, or unforeseen circumstances.

(b) This prohibition is consistent with PDDC §12.6 and applies to Sectors as constituent units of the Commonwealth.

(c) Disruptions to Sector operations are addressed through the FSM fault handler protocol at the appropriate level — Sector or Commonwealth — and through ordinary Sector governance processes.

(d) A Sector that believes it is at risk of a fault may petition the Constitutional Court for a pre-emptive state review. This is the correct mechanism. Emergency provisions are not.


# TITLE 3 - TREASURY INTEGRATION

## PART 6 - Sector Financial Obligations

### §6.1 - Treasury Delegate Representation

(a) Each Sector holds Treasury Assembly delegate representation according to its Tier classification per PDDC §1.1 of the PDDC-TREASURY-AND-FUNDING instrument.

(b) Treasury delegates are elected by the Sector Assembly for one-year terms.

(c) Treasury delegates report back to the SA at each quarterly meeting on TA proceedings.

### §6.2 - Annual Financial Reporting

(a) Each Sector submits an Annual Financial Report to the Treasury Assembly by the first day of Month 2 of the Aevoric fiscal year.

(b) The Annual Financial Report covers: total revenues and expenditures; ISRU royalty payments if applicable; Workers' Dividend contributions; and Commons Reserve remittances.

(c) Annual Financial Reports are published via FTS/OTF-1.

### §6.3 - ISRU Revenue Reporting

(a) ISRU-active Sectors submit quarterly ISRU Revenue Reports to the Treasury Assembly per PDDC-GOVERNANCE-MECHANICS §8.3(d).

(b) Quarterly ISRU Revenue Reports are published via FTS/OTF-1.


# TITLE 4 - CHARTER COMPLIANCE AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

## PART 7 - Charter Compliance Procedures

### §7.1 - Compliance Review Triggers

(a) Constitutional Court compliance review of a Sector Charter may be triggered by:

(1) Initial ratification submission under §4.1;

(2) Amendment submission under §4.2;

(3) Quinquennial review under §4.3;

(4) Member petition alleging non-compliance;

(5) Governance Secretariat referral upon detecting a State Registry anomaly attributable to Charter provisions; or

(6) Capture Tribunal referral in the course of capture proceedings.

### §7.2 - Compliance Review Procedure

(a) The Constitutional Court acknowledges compliance review requests within forty-eight (48) hours.

(b) The Court issues an initial compliance assessment within fourteen (14) days of receiving the Charter or the amendment.

(c) The Sector is given thirty (30) days to respond to any compliance concerns identified in the initial assessment.

(d) The Court issues a final compliance decision within sixty (60) days of the submission.

(e) Compliance decisions are published via FTS/OTF-1.

### §7.3 - Dispute Resolution

(a) Disputes between Sectors are referred to the Constitutional Court by either Sector or by the CC.

(b) Disputes between a Sector and the Commonwealth are heard by the full Constitutional Court.

(c) Disputes between Sector Members and Sector leadership that cannot be resolved internally are brought to the Constitutional Court.

(d) No Sector Charter may require internal dispute resolution as a prerequisite to Constitutional Court access.

(a) The SA elects a Deputy Presiding Officer on the same cycle as the Presiding Officer.

### §C.5 - Treasury Delegate

# TITLE 6 - SECTOR AVIA REFERENCE CHARTER

## PART 9 - Sector Avia Charter

### §9.1 - Name and Domain

(a) This Sector is known as Sector Avia — the Aviation, Aerospace, and Aeronautics Sector of the Aevoric Commonwealth.

(b) Sector Avia is the First Sector of the Aevoric Commonwealth, established under the Keefeian Compact on Day 1, Year 1 A.E. (20 May 2026 C.E.).

(c) Sector Avia's domain encompasses: atmospheric and orbital aviation; aerospace engineering, research, and development; aeronautics standards and certification; space transportation and launch operations; and the cultural heritage of human and Avian flight in all forms.

(d) The name Aevoria itself derives from aevum (Latin: eternity, epoch) and avis (Latin: bird, root of aviation). Sector Avia is therefore not merely an operational Sector — it is the embodiment of the Commonwealth's foundational cultural identity.

### §9.2 - Purpose

(a) Sector Avia exists to:

(1) Advance aviation, aerospace, and aeronautics as the primary technological and cultural expression of the Aevoric Commonwealth;

(2) Develop and maintain the Commonwealth's flight infrastructure, including atmospheric routes, orbital lanes, and interplanetary trajectories;

(3) Establish and enforce aeronautics safety and certification standards across Commonwealth operations;

(4) Preserve and advance the cultural heritage of flight as the defining metaphor and practice of Aevoric civilization; and

(5) Serve as the primary Commonwealth interface with the CUR on matters of aerospace governance, flight safety, and space resource utilization.

(b) Sector Avia embodies Per Avia, Ad Astra — Through Flight, To the Stars — in operational form.

### §9.3 - FSM Identity

(a) Sector Avia acknowledges the FSM governance architecture of PDDC TITLE 5 in full.

(b) Sector Avia understands that its domain — aviation — is itself a safety-critical system. The FSM governance model is therefore not merely a constitutional obligation for Sector Avia; it is culturally resonant. As aviation systems make catastrophic states unreachable by design, Sector Avia's governance makes destructive institutional states unreachable by design.

(c) Sector Avia will maintain valid operational states at all times, cooperate fully with fault handler protocols, and never adopt governance instruments approaching forbidden transitions.

### §9.4 - Membership

(a) Full Avian Membership in Sector Avia is open to any Full Avian PDDC Member who works, studies, or holds active interest in aviation, aerospace, aeronautics, or the cultural heritage of flight.

(b) No additional eligibility requirements beyond Full Avian PDDC Membership and a declared interest in Sector Avia's domain apply.

### §9.5 - Governance

(a) Sector Avia is governed by a Sector Assembly, a Presiding Officer, a Deputy Presiding Officer, a Sector Tribunal, and a Treasury Delegate, all constituted per the Model Charter template of TITLE 5 of this instrument.

(b) The Presiding Officer of Sector Avia holds the advisory seat at GA Ordinary Sessions per PDDC-GOVERNANCE-MECHANICS §10.5(b).

(c) Sector Avia maintains the Aevoric cultural heritage archives per PDDC-GOVERNANCE-MECHANICS §10.5(c), including:

(1) The etymological record of the name Aevoria and its aviation derivation;
