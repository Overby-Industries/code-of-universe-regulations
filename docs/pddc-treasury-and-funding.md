# TITLE 0 - PDDC Treasury and Funding — Financial Architecture of the Aevoric Commonwealth

## PART 0 - The Aevoric Commonwealth (Aevoria)

### §0.1 - Treasury Assembly and General Assembly — Issued under PDDC §9.2 and §18.3 Effective: 

Day 1, Year 1 Aevoric Era — 20 May 2026 C.E.

(a) Status: Binding on all financial operations of the Aevoric Commonwealth

### §0.2 - Preamble

This instrument governs the financial architecture of the Aevoric Commonwealth under the Progressive Direct Democracy Constitution. It establishes the composition, authority, and procedures of the Treasury Assembly; defines revenue sources including ISRU economy contributions; sets mandatory expenditure floors for rights-protective allocations; regulates deficit, surplus, and reserve management; and establishes audit, transparency, and anti-corruption standards consistent with the PDDC's core commitments.

A treasury is not a vault. It is a circulatory system — the health of the whole depends on how well it flows. Finance is not a neutral technical domain. It is where values are expressed as choices. This instrument reflects the PDDC's commitment to material equity, commons stewardship, democratic accountability, and the rejection of financial capture.

Every provision of this instrument is read alongside the RALC, which imposes binding obligations on how the treasury serves all life, not merely those with formal membership status. Every provision is also read alongside PDDC TITLE 5 — the FSM governance architecture — which governs how the treasury operates during fault conditions and Protected Mode. The treasury does not have emergency powers. It does not gain expanded authority during disruption. It continues to operate, unchanged, within its ordinary constitutional mandate.

Per Avia, Ad Astra.


# TITLE 1 - TREASURY ASSEMBLY

## PART 1 - Composition and Selection

### §1.1 - Treasury Assembly Composition

The Treasury Assembly (TA) is the primary financial governance body of the Aevoric Commonwealth. It is composed of the following seats:

(a) Seven (7) Member Representatives elected by full GA membership via Ranked Choice Voting for two-year staggered terms;

(b) Sector Treasury Delegates at a ratio of one (1) per Tier-1 Sector, one (1) per two (2) Tier-2 Sectors, and one (1) shared per three (3) Tier-3 Sectors, elected by each Sector Assembly for one-year terms;

(c) One (1) ISRU Revenue Steward elected by Members holding ISRU licenses from a pool of non-license-holders for a two-year term;

(d) One (1) RALC Finance Liaison appointed by the RALC Standing Board for a two-year term;

(e) One (1) Independent Fiscal Auditor selected by the Constitutional Court from an external pool for a three-year term, non-voting; and

(f) One (1) Workers' Dividend Representative elected by Guild of Workers' Standards membership for a two-year term.

### §1.2 - Eligibility

(a) Any Full Avian Member in good standing with at least twelve (12) months of membership and no active Tribunal proceedings may stand for elected TA seats.

(b) TA Members may not simultaneously hold CC executive roles.

(c) Members with undisclosed conflicts of interest are ineligible.

(d) The ISRU Revenue Steward must be a non-license-holder to prevent capture of ISRU revenue oversight by license interests.

### §1.3 - Term Limits

No individual may serve more than three (3) consecutive terms in any single TA seat. After a one-term break, re-election is permitted.

### §1.4 - Chair and Deputy Chair

(a) The TA elects a Chair and Deputy Chair from among its voting Members at the first meeting of each fiscal year.

(b) The Chair convenes meetings, represents the TA before the GA and CC, and certifies all Treasury reports.

(c) The Deputy Chair assumes Chair duties during vacancy, incapacity, or conflict of interest.

(d) Neither the Chair nor the Deputy Chair holds authority beyond the TA's collective mandate.


## PART 2 - Authority and Jurisdiction

### §2.1 - TA Authority

The Treasury Assembly holds authority over:

(a) Preparation of the Annual Budget for GA approval;

(b) Administration of the Commons Reserve per PDDC §9.3;

(c) Issuance, suspension, and revocation of ISRU licenses per PDDC §9.2;

(d) Collection and distribution of ISRU royalties;

(e) Management of the Protected Mode Continuity Reserve per §6.3 of this instrument;

(f) Financial reporting and transparency per TITLE 6 of this instrument; and

(g) Financial compliance review of Sector Annual Financial Reports.

### §2.2 - Limits on TA Authority

(a) The TA may not exceed the authority delegated by the GA in the Annual Budget.

(b) The TA may not redirect budget allocations without GA authorization, except for technical corrections within existing line items not exceeding five percent (5%) of the affected allocation.

(c) The TA holds no legislative authority and may not create enforceable obligations on Members beyond those authorized by the GA.

(d) The TA may not expand its own authority during Protected Mode. See §6.1 for Protected Mode financial rules.

### §2.3 - TA Quorum and Voting

(a) Quorum for TA meetings requires a majority of voting Members.

(b) Ordinary TA decisions require a majority of voting Members present.

(c) License suspension or revocation requires a two-thirds (2/3) TA vote.

(d) Budget submission to the GA requires a two-thirds (2/3) TA vote.

(e) All TA votes are recorded and published via FTS/OTF-1 within forty-eight (48) hours.


# TITLE 2 - REVENUE ARCHITECTURE

## PART 3 - ISRU Revenue System

### §3.1 - ISRU Revenue Classification

ISRU revenues are classified into the following categories for accounting and distribution purposes:

(a) Extraction Royalties — fees paid by license holders on the gross extraction value of all in-situ resources extracted under license, calculated as a percentage defined in each license agreement and no lower than the Commonwealth Royalty Floor established by §3.2;

(b) Processing Fees — fees for use of Commonwealth processing infrastructure where applicable;

(c) License Fees — one-time and renewal fees for ISRU license issuance; and

(d) Environmental Compliance Fees — fees assessed for remediation obligations arising from extraction activities.

### §3.2 - Commonwealth Royalty Floor

(a) The Commonwealth Royalty Floor is the minimum royalty rate applicable to all ISRU licenses, regardless of negotiated terms.

(b) The Floor is established by the TA at the start of each fiscal year and may not be set below eight percent (8%) of gross extraction value.

(c) The Floor applies to all existing and new licenses and may not be waived for any license holder.

(d) Proposed reductions to the Floor below eight percent (8%) are Type B constitutional amendments requiring GA supermajority and membership referendum per PDDC §15.2(b).

### §3.3 - Revenue Distribution Waterfall

All ISRU revenues are distributed in the following mandatory order. No step may be skipped and no step's allocation may be reduced to fund a later step:

(a) Step 1 — Commons Reserve: Twenty percent (20%) of gross ISRU revenue is transferred to the Commons Reserve before any other distribution. This is the Commons Reserve Floor established by PDDC §9.3(a).

(b) Step 2 — Environmental Remediation Fund: Three percent (3%) of gross ISRU revenue is transferred to the Environmental Remediation Fund for restoration of extraction sites.

(c) Step 3 — Workers' Dividend Pool: Five percent (5%) of gross ISRU revenue is transferred to the Workers' Dividend Pool for annual distribution to ISRU workers per §3.4.

(d) Step 4 — License Holder Returns: Remaining revenue is returned to the license holder, subject to any additional negotiated obligations.

### §3.4 - Workers' Dividend Distribution

(a) The Workers' Dividend Pool is distributed annually to all individuals who performed ISRU work during the preceding fiscal year, regardless of their membership category.

(b) The distribution formula is proposed annually by the Workers' Dividend Representative and adopted by TA majority vote.

(c) The formula must distribute no less than sixty percent (60%) of the Pool on an equal per-capita basis to all eligible workers.

(d) The Workers' Dividend distribution is published via FTS/OTF-1 within fourteen (14) days of distribution.

### §3.5 - ISRU License Framework

(a) ISRU licenses are issued by the TA following a documented review process covering:

(1) Operational plan and resource scope;

(2) Environmental impact assessment;

(3) Labor standards compliance plan;

(4) Commons Reserve remittance schedule;

(5) Insurance and bonding requirements; and

(6) FSM compliance declaration acknowledging that the licensee will not use ISRU operations to approach forbidden states under PDDC §12.3.

(b) License terms are a minimum of one (1) year and a maximum of ten (10) years per issuance, renewable.

(c) License categories:

(1) Exploratory License — for survey, mapping, and sampling activities, with no extraction permitted;

(2) Extraction License — for full extraction operations, subject to the full royalty schedule;

(3) Processing License — for refinement and processing of materials extracted under another license; and

(4) Integrated License — for combined extraction and processing under a single licensee.

(d) Licenses are public documents published via FTS/OTF-1 within forty-eight (48) hours of issuance.


## PART 4 - Non-ISRU Revenue

### §4.1 - Membership Contributions

(a) The GA may establish mandatory Member contributions (dues) as a Commonwealth revenue source.

(b) Dues schedules must be graduated based on Member capacity and may not impose equivalent flat rates on Members with disparate resource access.

(c) Dues collection may not condition Member participation rights. Non-payment of dues triggers an administrative review process, not suspension of PDDC rights.

### §4.2 - External Revenue

(a) The Commonwealth may enter into revenue-generating agreements with external entities, including CUR-member organizations and external parties.

(b) External revenue agreements are negotiated by the CC and approved by TA majority vote and GA resolution.

(c) No external revenue agreement may:

(1) Grant external entities governance rights or influence over Commonwealth institutions;

(2) Require any Avian or Recognized Life-Form to waive RALC rights;

(3) Create financial obligations that would constitute a forbidden transition under PDDC §12.3; or

(4) Subordinate any Commonwealth financial function to external control.

### §4.3 - Grants and Donations

(a) The Commonwealth may accept grants and donations from external sources subject to TA review.

(b) No grant or donation may be accepted that imposes governance conditions, naming rights over public institutions, or preferential treatment for the donor in any Commonwealth proceeding.

(c) Grants and donations above a threshold established by the TA are reported to the GA and published via FTS/OTF-1.


# TITLE 3 - EXPENDITURE FRAMEWORK

## PART 5 - Mandatory Expenditure Floors

### §5.1 - Commons Reserve Sub-Account Floors

The Commons Reserve established by PDDC §9.3 is subdivided into the following mandatory sub-accounts with minimum allocation floors. These floors may not be reduced by TA action; reduction requires a Type B constitutional amendment:

(a) Life-Sustaining Infrastructure Fund — minimum forty percent (40%) of Commons Reserve receipts: funds maintenance and expansion of all infrastructure essential to Avian survival, health, and habitat;

(b) Universal Basic Provisions Fund — minimum twenty percent (20%) of Commons Reserve receipts: funds universal access to food, water, shelter, and basic communications for all Avians and Recognized Life-Forms;

(c) RALC Obligations Fund — minimum fifteen percent (15%) of Commons Reserve receipts: funds Commonwealth fulfillment of RALC obligations to all Recognized Life-Forms;

(d) Long-Term Civilizational Investment Fund — minimum fifteen percent (15%) of Commons Reserve receipts: funds research, education, culture, and long-horizon infrastructure for the Commonwealth's Ascending flight path; and

(e) Protected Mode Continuity Reserve — minimum ten percent (10%) of Commons Reserve receipts: funds operational continuity during Protected Mode activations under PDDC §12.5.

### §5.2 - Rights Expenditure Floor

(a) No less than five percent (5%) of the total Annual Budget shall be allocated to rights enforcement and legal access programs, including Constitutional Court operations, Capture Tribunal operations, and Member legal assistance.

(b) This floor ensures that the financial means to enforce rights are not subordinated to other budget priorities.

(c) The rights enforcement floor may not be reduced by budget amendment without a two-thirds (2/3) GA vote.

### §5.3 - Governance Operations Floor

(a) No less than three percent (3%) of the total Annual Budget shall be allocated to governance operations, including GA Secretariat functions, CC operations, FTS/OTF-1 maintenance, and State Registry operations.

(b) Underfunding governance operations is a capture risk. The floor ensures that governance infrastructure cannot be defunded as a means of disabling democratic function.

### §5.4 - RALC Compliance Floor

(a) No less than two percent (2%) of the total Annual Budget shall be allocated to RALC compliance activities, including RALC Finance Liaison operations, compliance reporting, and obligations to Recognized Life-Forms.

(b) The RALC compliance floor ensures that ethical obligations to all life are not treated as discretionary.


## PART 6 - Protected Mode Financial Architecture

### §6.1 - Protected Mode Financial Continuity Principle

(a) The Aevoric Commonwealth does not have emergency financial powers and does not recognize emergency financial authority. These concepts are architecturally absent from this instrument, consistent with PDDC §12.6.

(b) When a fault is declared under PDDC §12.4 and Protected Mode is activated, the financial architecture of the Commonwealth does not change. The TA continues to perform all ordinary functions within its ordinary constitutional mandate.

(c) No individual, institution, or coalition gains any financial authority beyond their ordinary constitutional mandate during Protected Mode. Financial authority does not expand during Protected Mode for any reason.

(d) Any instrument purporting to expand financial authority during Protected Mode is an attempted forbidden transition under PDDC §12.3(a) and triggers the fault handler of PDDC §12.4.

### §6.2 - Continuity of Budget Operations

(a) During Protected Mode activation, the most recently adopted Annual Budget remains in full force without modification.

(b) All scheduled disbursements proceed normally on their ordinary schedule.

(c) All ISRU royalty collection, Commons Reserve transfers, and Workers' Dividend Pool contributions proceed normally.

(d) No budget line may be redirected, suspended, or expanded on the basis of Protected Mode status.

(e) The TA Chair issues a Financial Continuity Notice within twenty-four (24) hours of Protected Mode activation, confirming the status of each item in this section. The Notice is public and published via FTS/OTF-1.

### §6.3 - Protected Mode Continuity Reserve

(a) The Protected Mode Continuity Reserve is a dedicated sub-account of the Commons Reserve holding a minimum of ninety (90) days of life-sustaining disbursements.

(b) The Reserve is maintained separately from operating funds and is not accessible for ordinary expenditures.

(c) The Reserve is accessible only for the following disbursements during Protected Mode activation:

(1) Life-Sustaining Infrastructure Fund disbursements where the ordinary disbursement mechanism is disrupted by the fault triggering Protected Mode;

(2) Universal Basic Provisions Fund disbursements under the same circumstances; and

(3) RALC Obligations Fund disbursements under the same circumstances.

(d) Reserve drawdowns are reported to the Constitutional Court and published via FTS/OTF-1 within twenty-four (24) hours.

(e) The Reserve is replenished to its minimum threshold within ninety (90) days of Protected Mode resolution.

### §6.4 - Prohibition on Financial Emergency Instruments

(a) No financial instrument, budget amendment, TA resolution, CC Coordinating Directive, or GA legislation may create, invoke, or give effect to:

(1) Emergency financial authority;

(2) Emergency budget reallocation powers;

(3) Suspension of budget floors on the basis of urgency, threat, crisis, or necessity;

(4) Expansion of TA, CC, or any other body's financial authority during disruption; or

(5) Any functional equivalent of the above, regardless of terminology.

(b) Instruments violating this section are void ab initio and constitute attempted forbidden transitions under PDDC §12.3(a).

(c) The absence of emergency financial authority is a feature, not a gap. The Protected Mode Continuity Reserve is the pre-positioned, pre-authorized mechanism for continuity. No additional authority is needed or permitted.


# TITLE 4 - BUDGET PROCESS

## PART 7 - Annual Budget Cycle

### §7.1 - Fiscal Year

(a) The Aevoric Commonwealth fiscal year runs from Day 1 of Year N A.E. through the last day of Year N A.E., aligned with the calendar year of the Aevoric Era.

(b) The first fiscal year runs from Day 1, Year 1 A.E. through the last day of Year 1 A.E.

### §7.2 - Budget Preparation

(a) The TA begins Annual Budget preparation no later than Month 10 of the fiscal year for which the budget will apply.

(b) The TA solicits budget requests from:

(1) All Commonwealth institutions including the GA Secretariat, CC, CoC, and Capture Tribunal;

(2) All Sectors via their Treasury Delegates;

(3) All Guilds; and

(4) Working Groups with multi-year mandates.

(c) Budget requests are published via FTS/OTF-1 within fourteen (14) days of receipt.

(d) A forty-five (45) day public comment period follows publication of all budget requests.

### §7.3 - Draft Budget

(a) The TA compiles a Draft Annual Budget no later than Month 11 of the fiscal year.

(b) The Draft Budget must satisfy all mandatory expenditure floors of TITLE 3.

(c) The Draft Budget is submitted to the CC for review and comment within seven (7) days of compilation.

(d) The CC has thirty (30) days to review and submit written comments. CC comments are advisory; the TA is not bound to adopt them.

(e) The Final Draft Budget incorporating TA responses to CC comments is submitted to the GA no later than the first day of Month 12.

### §7.4 - GA Budget Adoption

(a) The GA has thirty (30) days to deliberate on the Final Draft Budget.

(b) The GA adopts the Annual Budget by simple majority vote.

(c) The GA may amend the Draft Budget subject to the following constraints:

(1) No amendment may reduce any mandatory expenditure floor below its constitutional or instrument minimum;

(2) No amendment may reallocate funds from the Protected Mode Continuity Reserve; and

(3) No amendment may expand any institution's authority beyond its constitutional mandate.

(d) If the GA fails to adopt a budget by the last day of the fiscal year, the prior year's budget continues on a month-by-month basis with no expenditure increases until a new budget is adopted.

(e) Month-by-month continuation does not authorize new expenditure categories or capital commitments.

### §7.5 - Mid-Year Budget Adjustments

(a) The TA may propose mid-year budget adjustments to the GA for any fiscal year.

(b) Mid-year adjustments require a GA majority vote.

(c) Mid-year adjustments may not reduce mandatory expenditure floors.

(d) Mid-year adjustments arising from Protected Mode activation may not expand any institution's authority.


# TITLE 5 - DEFICIT, SURPLUS, AND RESERVE MANAGEMENT

## PART 8 - Deficit Management

### §8.1 - Deficit Definition and Threshold

(a) A deficit exists when total Commonwealth expenditures in a fiscal year exceed total revenues.

(b) A Moderate Deficit is a deficit of up to five percent (5%) of total budgeted revenue.

(c) A Material Deficit is a deficit exceeding five percent (5%) of total budgeted revenue.

### §8.2 - Deficit Response

(a) A Moderate Deficit triggers a mandatory TA review and remediation plan submitted to the GA within sixty (60) days of fiscal year close.

(b) A Material Deficit triggers a mandatory TA review and remediation plan plus a mandatory GA extraordinary session within thirty (30) days.

(c) Remediation plans must achieve structural balance within three (3) fiscal years without reducing mandatory expenditure floors.

(d) Remediation may not include suspension of the Protected Mode Continuity Reserve.

### §8.3 - Borrowing

(a) The Commonwealth may borrow to address deficits with GA authorization by two-thirds (2/3) vote.

(b) Borrowing may not exceed twenty percent (20%) of annual budgeted revenue in aggregate outstanding principal.

(c) Borrowing terms must be publicly disclosed via FTS/OTF-1.

(d) No borrowing arrangement may grant lenders governance rights or preferential treatment in Commonwealth proceedings.


## PART 9 - Surplus Management

### §9.1 - Surplus Disposition

(a) A surplus exists when total Commonwealth revenues in a fiscal year exceed total expenditures.

(b) The first thirty percent (30%) of any surplus is transferred to the Protected Mode Continuity Reserve until the Reserve reaches its ninety (90) day minimum threshold.

(c) After the Continuity Reserve is fully funded, surplus funds are allocated by the TA as follows:

(1) A minimum of fifty percent (50%) to the Long-Term Civilizational Investment Fund;

(2) A minimum of twenty-five percent (25%) to the Universal Basic Provisions Fund; and

(3) The remainder by TA vote, subject to GA confirmation.

(d) No surplus may be distributed as individual dividends to Members. Surplus funds serve collective purposes only.

### §9.2 - Strategic Reserves

(a) The TA may establish Strategic Reserves for specific long-term purposes with GA authorization.

(b) Strategic Reserves are governed by the TA within the purposes authorized by the GA.

(c) Strategic Reserves may not be raided for ordinary operating expenses without GA approval.


# TITLE 6 - AUDIT AND TRANSPARENCY

## PART 10 - Independent Fiscal Auditor

### §10.1 - Role and Independence

(a) The Independent Fiscal Auditor (IFA) is a non-voting TA Member selected by the Constitutional Court from an external pool of qualified financial auditors with no prior affiliation with the Commonwealth.

(b) The IFA serves a three-year term and is not subject to TA direction in the performance of audit functions.

(c) The IFA reports simultaneously to the TA, the CC, the GA, and the Constitutional Court.

(d) The IFA's independence is an anti-capture safeguard. Any attempt by any institution to direct, constrain, or penalize the IFA in the performance of audit functions is a capture attempt under PDDC §13.1 and triggers the fault handler of PDDC §12.4.

### §10.2 - Annual Audit

(a) The IFA conducts a comprehensive Annual Audit of all Commonwealth financial operations.

(b) The Annual Audit covers:

(1) Compliance with all mandatory expenditure floors of TITLE 3;

(2) ISRU royalty collection completeness;

(3) Commons Reserve sub-account balances and disbursements;

(4) Protected Mode Continuity Reserve balance;

(5) Workers' Dividend distribution;

(6) Budget adherence;

(7) Deficit or surplus characterization; and

(8) All Sector Annual Financial Reports.

(c) The Annual Audit report is published via FTS/OTF-1 within ninety (90) days of fiscal year close.

### §10.3 - FSM Financial Monitoring

(a) The IFA includes in the Annual Audit a review of all financial transactions during any Protected Mode period, confirming:

(1) No budget reallocations occurred on the basis of Protected Mode status;

(2) All disbursements proceeded within ordinary budget authority;

(3) Protected Mode Continuity Reserve drawdowns, if any, were authorized and properly documented; and

(4) No financial instrument functioned as emergency financial authority.

(b) This FSM Financial Monitoring component of the Annual Audit is published as a standalone section prominently accessible via FTS/OTF-1.

### §10.4 - Quarterly Financial Reports

(a) The TA publishes Quarterly Financial Reports covering revenues, expenditures, ISRU royalty receipts, Commons Reserve balances, and Continuity Reserve status.

(b) Quarterly reports are published via FTS/OTF-1 within thirty (30) days of each quarter's close.

(c) Quarterly reports are submitted to the GA at the first Ordinary GA Session following publication.


## PART 11 - Anti-Capture Financial Safeguards

### §11.1 - Financial Capture Prevention

(a) Financial capture — the concentration of financial authority, resource control, or revenue access in any individual, faction, Sector, or external entity to a degree that subordinates the Commonwealth's financial function — is a form of capture under PDDC §13.1 and a forbidden transition under PDDC §12.3.

(b) Financial capture attempts include but are not limited to:

(1) Accumulation of ISRU licenses sufficient to dominate Commons Reserve revenue flows;

(2) Systematic underfunding of the rights enforcement floor to disable constitutional enforcement;

(3) Capturing the IFA selection process to install a compliant auditor;

(4) Using deficit conditions to justify suspension of mandatory expenditure floors;

(5) Directing the Protected Mode Continuity Reserve for non-continuity purposes; and

(6) Creating financial structures that give external entities functional control over Commonwealth expenditure.

### §11.2 - ISRU License Concentration Limit

(a) No individual, entity, or affiliated group of entities may hold ISRU licenses covering more than twenty-five percent (25%) of the Commonwealth's total licensed extraction volume in any single resource category.

(b) The concentration limit is reviewed annually by the IFA and reported to the TA and GA.

(c) Excess concentration triggers a mandatory TA review and divestiture or license transfer plan within sixty (60) days.

### §11.3 - Conflict of Interest in Financial Proceedings

(a) All TA Members must disclose all financial interests, ISRU relationships, and organizational affiliations that could create a conflict of interest in any matter before the TA.

(b) A TA Member with a disclosed conflict may not vote on the matter to which the conflict applies.

(c) Undisclosed conflicts discovered after a vote are referred to the Capture Tribunal.

(d) The IFA reviews conflict of interest disclosures as part of the Annual Audit.


# TITLE 7 - SECTOR FINANCIAL INTEGRATION

## PART 12 - Sector Financial Obligations

### §12.1 - Sector Annual Financial Reports

(a) Each Sector submits an Annual Financial Report to the TA by the first day of Month 2 of each Aevoric fiscal year.

(b) Annual Financial Reports cover: total revenues and expenditures; ISRU royalty payments if applicable; Workers' Dividend Pool contributions; Commons Reserve remittances; and conflict of interest disclosures.

(c) Annual Financial Reports are published via FTS/OTF-1 within fourteen (14) days of submission.

### §12.2 - Sector Non-Compliance

(a) Sectors failing to submit Annual Financial Reports by the deadline are flagged in the State Registry and notified by the TA Secretariat.

(b) Reports more than thirty (30) days late trigger a TA review and may result in suspension of Sector Treasury Assembly delegate voting rights until the report is submitted.

(c) Material financial violations discovered through Annual Financial Report review are referred to the IFA for investigation and potentially to the Capture Tribunal.

### §12.3 - Treasury Delegate Responsibilities

(a) Sector Treasury Delegates attend all TA meetings and vote on behalf of their Sector on TA matters.

(b) Treasury Delegates report to their Sector Assembly at each quarterly SA meeting.

(c) Treasury Delegates disclose all conflicts of interest per §11.3.


# TITLE 8 - CUR AND RALC FINANCIAL INTEGRATION

## PART 13 - CUR Financial Obligations

### §13.1 - CUR Assessments

(a) The Aevoric Commonwealth may be subject to CUR-level financial assessments for inter-organizational activities.

(b) CUR assessments are incorporated into the Annual Budget as a mandatory line item.

(c) No CUR assessment may require the Commonwealth to reduce mandatory expenditure floors, access the Protected Mode Continuity Reserve, or grant CUR governance rights over Commonwealth financial operations.

### §13.2 - Inter-Organizational Revenue Sharing

(a) Revenue sharing agreements with other CUR-member organizations require TA majority vote and GA confirmation.

(b) Revenue sharing agreements are published via FTS/OTF-1 within forty-eight (48) hours of ratification.


## PART 14 - RALC Financial Obligations

### §14.1 - RALC Finance Liaison Role

(a) The RALC Finance Liaison monitors all TA proceedings for RALC compliance and reports quarterly to the RALC Standing Board.

(b) The Liaison may flag any budget item or financial instrument as potentially inconsistent with RALC obligations, triggering a mandatory TA review.

(c) The Liaison's flags are published via FTS/OTF-1 within forty-eight (48) hours.

### §14.2 - RALC Obligations Floor

(a) The RALC Obligations Fund maintains the minimum fifteen percent (15%) of Commons Reserve receipts floor established by §5.1(c).

(b) Disbursements from the RALC Obligations Fund require documented connection to RALC obligations.

(c) The RALC Finance Liaison certifies RALC Obligations Fund disbursements as part of quarterly reporting.

### §14.3 - Non-Derogability of RALC Financial Obligations

(a) RALC financial obligations are non-derogable. No budget condition, deficit, Protected Mode activation, or any other governance circumstance reduces the Commonwealth's RALC financial obligations.

(b) If ordinary budget authority is insufficient to meet RALC obligations, the Protected Mode Continuity Reserve is accessible for RALC Obligations Fund disbursements per §6.3(c)(3).
