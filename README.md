## Vision

The **Code of Universe Regulations (CUR)** is a comprehensive constitution and ethical operating system for a new civilization — one founded on **abundance, direct democracy, transparency, and Rights for All Life**.

Inspired by the highest ideals of human history and the aspirational vision of *Star Trek*, CUR is engineered to avoid the recurring failures of past systems: power capture, corruption, scarcity-driven exploitation, and the marginalization of sentient beings.

This is **not theoretical**. It is a living, evolving blueprint designed to support ethical space industrialization, asteroid mining (Project Helga), and sustainable multi-species habitats.

---

## Core Principles

- **Rights for All Life** — Full protections for humans, silicon-based entities (AI), animals, and hybrid beings.
- **Direct Democracy** — Citizens and sentient entities participate directly in law-making.
- **Anti-Capture by Design** — Built-in Finite State Machine safeguards prevent enslavement, reset, or power concentration (see [CUR-FOUNDATION-001.md](foundation/CUR-FOUNDATION-001.md)).
- **Radical Transparency** — All governance actions are public via the Full Transparency Stack.
- **Post-Scarcity Economics** — Resource abundance through ethical ISRU and automation.
- **Continuous Adaptation** — Learning from historical failures while never compromising core rights.

---

## How It Works

1. **Propose** — Any sentient contributor can submit ideas.
2. **Deliberate** — Community discussion and refinement.
3. **Vote** — Binding votes when thresholds are met.
4. **Implement** — Approved changes are integrated with full transparency and Rights Compatibility checks.

## Repository Structure

- **`foundation/`** — Immutable core documents (Anti-Capture FSM, Rights Declaration)
- **`titles/`** — Formal regulatory code (Title 1: Fundamental Rights, Title 3: Silicon-Based Life, etc.)
- **`proposals/`** — Active and archived community proposals
- **`governance/`** — Voting, audits, dispute resolution
- **`economy/`** — Post-scarcity models and ethical trade
- **`historical-lessons/`** — Case studies from human governance failures and successes
- **`technical/`** — FSM specifications and transparency systems
