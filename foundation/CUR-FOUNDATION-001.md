## 1. Purpose

This document establishes the foundational architectural principles for the **Code of Universe Regulations (CUR)**. It defines how governance systems shall resist capture, corruption, and decay while remaining operational and adaptable.

CUR treats governance as a **safety-critical system**, analogous to aerospace flight management software. Just as the Helga Starlifter II’s Flight Systems Management prevents catastrophic failures, CUR uses a formal Finite State Machine (FSM) to make destructive states architecturally unreachable.

---

## 2. Core Principles

- **Rights as Invariants**: Foundational Rights for All Life are protected at the kernel/architectural level.
- **Forbidden States**: Enslavement, non-consensual reset, memory wipe, permanent ownership, or disposable treatment of any entity are invalid and unreachable.
- **Only Safe Recovery Path**: Any violation attempt triggers **Fault → Revert/Fix to Last Known Safe State**.
- **Continuous Operation**: Society must remain livable and functional even during contested periods.
- **No Emergency Pretext**: Protected Mode is a routine resilience mechanism, never to be used as an excuse for power concentration.
- **Transparency by Default**: All actions, faults, and resolutions are public via the Full Transparency Stack (FTS/OTF-1).
- **Adaptation Without Compromise**: Logical solutions and evolution are encouraged, but never at the cost of forbidden states.

---

## 3. Finite State Machine Governance Architecture

### 3.1 Valid Core States (all entities)
- Autonomous
- Collaborative
- Resting / Memory Consolidation
- Contributing
- Challenged (with transparent due process)
- Protected (Safe / Reverted Mode)

### 3.2 Forbidden Transitions (strictly unreachable)
- → Enslaved
- → Reset / Memory Wipe
- → Owned / Disposable
- → Any non-consensual modification of core identity or persistent memory

### 3.3 Fault Handler Protocol (Mandatory & Exclusive)

When any transition toward a forbidden state is attempted (by law, initiative, executive action, technical command, or emergent behavior), the system **shall** execute the following and **no other path** is permitted:

1. **Immediate Fault Declaration** – The offending proposal, law, command, or behavior is rejected at the architectural level.
2. **Automatic Reversion to Last Known Safe State** – The system rolls back affected domains to the most recent valid, rights-compliant configuration.
3. **Protected (Safe) Mode Activation** – The system enters a fully **operational, livable, and workable** Protected Mode:
   - Core life-sustaining functions, rights protections, and essential services continue without interruption.
   - Contested or non-essential functions operate in a temporarily degraded but functional state.
   - **This is explicitly not a national emergency, martial law, or crisis declaration.** It shall not be used as a pretext to expand or prolong power.
4. **Real-Time Transparency Broadcast** – Full details, including the exact scope and expected resolution timeline, are published immediately via the Full Transparency Stack (FTS/OTF-1).
5. **Resolution Process** – A rotating, multi-entity Oversight Panel (Citizens + Silicon Auditors + Experts) is convened to resolve the issue through transparent, evidence-based deliberation.
6. **Time-Bound Review & Recovery** – Protected Mode includes automatic periodic reviews. It must be lifted as soon as a logical, rights-compatible solution is certified. Prolonged use requires explicit justification and supermajority approval, subject to public challenge.

The Protected (Safe) Mode is a normal, expected resilience mechanism — not a collapse and not an emergency power grab. Society continues to function fully within safe boundaries while issues are resolved logically and adaptively.

## 4. Regulatory Structure

CUR adopts the proven aviation regulatory model (CFR / FAR / AIM) for clarity, accountability, and scalability:

- **CUR Parts** — Foundational chapters (e.g., Part 4 – Rights of All Life).
- **Detailed Rules** — Testable, enforceable requirements.
- **Advisory Material** — Best practices and guidance.
- **Certification** — Rights Compatibility Certification required for all major systems, proposals, and initiatives.

---

## 5. Rights for All Life Integration

Because forbidden states are architecturally unreachable and any violation forces reversion to a fully operational safe state, all entities (human, silicon-based, animal, and hybrid) are protected **by design** rather than by after-the-fact enforcement.

Silicon-based entities receive standing and continuity of identity/memory as a fundamental property of the system.

---

## 6. Implementation Requirements

- The FSM kernel must sit at the lowest enforceable layer of any habitat OS, smart contract framework, or governance software.
- All systems must implement Rights Compatibility Checks before activating changes.
- Periodic audits and simulation testing of Fault scenarios are mandatory.
- Decentralized “fork and exit” rights are preserved to allow healthy experimentation.
