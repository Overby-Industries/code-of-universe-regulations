# TITLE 0 - PDDC Governance Mechanics — Procedural Rules for All Commonwealth Institutions

## PART 0 - The Aevoric Commonwealth (Aevoria)

### §0.1 - Coordinating Council and Constitutional Court — Issued under PDDC §18.3 Effective: 

Day 1, Year 1 Aevoric Era — 20 May 2026 C.E.

(a) Status: Binding on all elected officials, appointed officers, Members exercising governance rights, and entities participating in Aevoric governance

### §0.2 - Preamble

This document establishes the detailed procedural rules for every governance institution created by the Progressive Direct Democracy Constitution of the Aevoric Commonwealth. Where the Constitution establishes the what and why of each institution, this document establishes the how. It is binding on all elected officials, appointed officers, Members exercising governance rights, and entities participating in Commonwealth governance.

Democracy without mechanics is aspiration. Mechanics without democracy is machinery. Together, implemented through the transparent and accountable institutions of the Aevoric Commonwealth, they are governance.

Procedural rules may be amended by the Coordinating Council with Constitutional Court concurrence, except where this document designates specific provisions as requiring General Assembly approval. No procedural rule may contradict the Constitution or the RALC. No procedural rule may create, invoke, or functionally approximate emergency powers, emergency authority, or any form of authority expansion during disruption. Such rules are void under PDDC §12.6 and constitute attempted forbidden transitions under PDDC §12.3.

The FSM governance architecture of PDDC TITLE 5 governs all procedural matters. Where a procedural rule produces a forbidden transition or enables capture, it is void regardless of the process by which it was adopted.


# TITLE 1 - GENERAL ASSEMBLY MECHANICS

## PART 1 - Sessions

### §1.1 - Ordinary Session Schedule

(a) Ordinary GA Sessions shall be convened in the first month of each fiscal quarter (Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4).

(b) The Governance Secretariat shall publish notice of each Ordinary Session no fewer than twenty-one (21) days in advance, including proposed agenda, all supporting documents, and participation instructions.

(c) Ordinary Sessions are listed in the Implementation Dashboard and the State Registry under the GA's Contributing operational state.

### §1.2 - Extraordinary Sessions

An Extraordinary Session may be convened:

(a) By petition of fifteen percent (15%) of Full Members, submitted to the Governance Secretariat, which must convene the session within fourteen (14) days of a valid petition;

(b) By unanimous consent of the Coordinating Council, with session convened within seven (7) days; or

(c) By order of the Constitutional Court in response to an FSM fault declaration requiring legislative action, under PDDC §12.4.

### §1.3 - Format and Accessibility

GA Sessions shall be conducted in a hybrid format accessible to all Members regardless of physical location. Accessibility requirements include:

(a) Real-time captioning and transcript availability;

(b) Interpretation services for recognized official languages of the Aevoric Commonwealth;

(c) Asynchronous participation windows of no fewer than forty-eight (48) hours for any matter requiring a vote, to accommodate Members across time zones and those with participation constraints; and

(d) Full session archive available within seventy-two (72) hours of session close via FTS/OTF-1.

### §1.4 - Agenda Setting

(a) The Coordinating Council proposes the agenda for each Ordinary Session at least twenty-one (21) days in advance.

(b) Any Member may submit agenda items for consideration.

(c) Agenda items submitted by petition of five percent (5%) of Full Members are guaranteed a place on the next available agenda.

(d) The CC may not remove a petitioned item without a two-thirds (2/3) CC vote accompanied by written public justification published via FTS/OTF-1.

### §1.5 - Order of Business

Unless varied by GA vote, the standard order of business is:

(a) Confirmation of quorum;

(b) Adoption of agenda;

(c) Previous session minutes approval;

(d) Reports from the Coordinating Council and Standing Bodies;

(e) FSM State Registry status report from the Governance Secretariat;

(f) Legislative business in order of agenda;

(g) Member-petitioned items;

(h) Open floor items admitted by vote; and

(i) Adjournment.

### §1.6 - Quorum and Voting Mechanics

(a) Quorum is established at the opening of each session and maintained by presence until adjournment.

(b) If quorum is lost during a session, pending votes are deferred to the next session.

(c) All votes are conducted by electronic or documented balloting and published within forty-eight (48) hours via FTS/OTF-1.

(d) Proxy voting is not permitted. Asynchronous voting within the permitted window counts as participation for quorum purposes.


## PART 2 - Proposals and Legislation

### §2.1 - Proposal Submission

(a) Any Full Avian Member may submit a proposal to the Governance Secretariat at any time.

(b) Proposals must include: the text of the proposed legislation or resolution; a statement of purpose; an analysis of constitutional compliance; and an FSM compliance declaration affirming that the proposal does not constitute or enable a forbidden transition under PDDC §12.3.

(c) The FSM compliance declaration is reviewed by the Constitutional Court within seven (7) days of submission. Proposals flagged as potential forbidden transitions are not cleared for approach until the CoC resolves the issue.

### §2.2 - Cleared for Approach Status

(a) A proposal is Cleared for Approach when the Constitutional Court confirms it presents no facially apparent forbidden transition risk.

(b) Cleared for Approach status does not constitute a substantive endorsement by the CoC.

(c) Proposals not yet reviewed remain in Resting state in the State Registry.

### §2.3 - Deliberation Period

(a) All Cleared for Approach proposals are published via FTS/OTF-1 with a minimum deliberation period of fourteen (14) days before a vote may be held.

(b) During the deliberation period, any Member may submit amendments, comments, or counterproposals via the Governance Secretariat.

(c) The deliberation period may be extended by CC vote or Member petition. It may not be shortened except by unanimous GA vote.

### §2.4 - Amendment During Deliberation

(a) Amendments to proposals during the deliberation period must be submitted with an updated FSM compliance declaration.

(b) Substantive amendments restart the deliberation period clock.

(c) Technical corrections certified by the Governance Secretariat do not restart the deliberation period.

### §2.5 - Enactment

(a) Legislation enacted by the GA is published via FTS/OTF-1 within forty-eight (48) hours of the vote.

(b) Legislation takes effect seventy-two (72) hours after publication unless the GA specifies a different effective date or the CoC issues a temporary stay pending constitutional review.

(c) Enacted legislation is assigned a permanent identifier and added to the Commonwealth Legislative Registry maintained by the Governance Secretariat.


## PART 3 - GA Officers and Committees

### §3.1 - GA Chair

(a) The GA elects a Chair from among Full Members who are not CC Members at the first session of each year.

(b) The GA Chair presides over GA sessions, manages the agenda, and certifies session records.

(c) The GA Chair holds no legislative authority and may not cast a deciding vote.

### §3.2 - Standing Committees

(a) The GA may establish Standing Committees for ongoing oversight of defined Commonwealth domains.

(b) Standing Committees are advisory unless the GA grants specific decision-making authority by supermajority.

(c) Standing Committee membership may not be restricted by Sector affiliation.

### §3.3 - Ad Hoc Committees

(a) Ad Hoc Committees may be established by the GA or CC for time-limited purposes.

(b) Ad Hoc Committees dissolve upon completion of their mandate or by GA resolution.


# TITLE 2 - COORDINATING COUNCIL MECHANICS

## PART 4 - CC Operations

### §4.1 - Meeting Schedule

(a) The CC meets in ordinary session no fewer than twice per month.

(b) Extraordinary sessions may be called by the Prime Convener or any three (3) CC Members.

(c) All CC sessions are conducted in a format accessible to all CC Members regardless of location.

(d) CC session summaries are published via FTS/OTF-1 within forty-eight (48) hours.

### §4.2 - Decision-Making

(a) CC decisions require a majority of Members present and voting unless a higher threshold is specified.

(b) Coordinating Directives require a majority vote.

(c) Decisions affecting the Implementation Roadmap require a two-thirds (2/3) CC vote.

(d) No CC decision may contradict GA legislation or a CoC ruling.

### §4.3 - Prime Convener Responsibilities

(a) The Prime Convener convenes and chairs CC sessions.

(b) The Prime Convener represents the Aevoric Commonwealth in inter-organizational proceedings under the CUR Master Framework.

(c) The Prime Convener certifies Coordinating Directives after CC vote.

(d) The Prime Convener may not act unilaterally on any matter requiring a CC majority.

(e) The Prime Convener reports to the GA at each Ordinary GA Session on CC activities.

### §4.4 - Coordinating Directives

(a) Coordinating Directives are the CC's primary implementing instrument.

(b) Each Directive must cite the specific GA legislation it implements and the authority delegated thereby.

(c) Directives are reviewed by the Governance Secretariat for constitutional compliance before publication.

(d) Any Directive that on its face purports to expand CC authority, create emergency powers, or approach any forbidden state under PDDC §12.3 is referred immediately to the Constitutional Court.

### §4.5 - Protected Mode Coordination Protocol

(a) During Protected Mode activation under PDDC §12.5, CC sessions continue on their ordinary schedule.

(b) The CC shall issue a Protected Mode Coordination Notice within twelve (12) hours of fault declaration under PDDC §12.4, specifying:

(1) The institution or domain affected;

(2) The reversion state to which affected domains have reverted;

(3) The ordinary CC functions continuing without change; and

(4) The timeline for Constitutional Court review.

(c) The Protected Mode Coordination Notice is a public document published via FTS/OTF-1. It does not create new CC authority. It is informational.

(d) The CC may not use Protected Mode Coordination Notices to expand its authority, redirect budget allocations, alter institutional composition, or modify any GA-enacted legislation.

(e) Any Protected Mode Coordination Notice that purports to do any of the above is void and constitutes an attempted forbidden transition under PDDC §12.3(a), triggering the fault handler of PDDC §12.4.


## PART 5 - CC Member Obligations

### §5.1 - Conflict of Interest

(a) CC Members must disclose all financial interests, organizational affiliations, and personal relationships that could create a conflict of interest in any matter before the CC.

(b) A CC Member with a disclosed conflict may not vote on the matter to which the conflict applies.

(c) Undisclosed conflicts discovered after a vote may be referred to the Capture Tribunal.

### §5.2 - Attendance and Participation

(a) CC Members are expected to attend all ordinary sessions.

(b) Members absent for more than three (3) consecutive ordinary sessions without documented cause are subject to a CC censure vote.

(c) Three (3) censures in a twelve-month period trigger an automatic GA confidence review.

### §5.3 - Information Obligations

(a) CC Members must share all material information relevant to CC deliberations with all other CC Members.

(b) No CC Member may withhold information that would affect another Member's vote.

(c) Violations of this obligation are grounds for Tribunal referral.


# TITLE 3 - CONSTITUTIONAL COURT MECHANICS

## PART 6 - Court Operations

### §6.1 - Session Structure

(a) The Constitutional Court operates continuously, with Justices assigned to rotating panels for different case categories.

(b) The Court publishes a public docket of all pending matters via FTS/OTF-1.

(c) Ordinary proceedings are conducted in full-panel or three-Justice panel configurations as appropriate to case type.

(d) FSM fault declarations, Protected Mode certification, and State Registry matters are heard by full panel.

### §6.2 - Case Intake

(a) Cases are submitted to the Governance Secretariat acting as Court registrar.

(b) The registrar acknowledges receipt within forty-eight (48) hours and assigns a docket number.

(c) FSM-related petitions are expedited and acknowledged within twenty-four (24) hours per PDDC §12.7(c).

### §6.3 - Deliberation and Decision

(a) The Court deliberates internally. Deliberation records are not public, but all decisions are published with full reasoning.

(b) Decisions on ordinary constitutional questions are issued within sixty (60) days of full briefing.

(c) Decisions on FSM fault declarations and Protected Mode matters are issued within forty-eight (48) hours of the triggering event.

(d) Rights claims are expedited and resolved within thirty (30) days of intake.

### §6.4 - Precedent and Registry

(a) All CoC decisions are permanent public records maintained in the Constitutional Registry.

(b) The Constitutional Registry is the authoritative record of last known safe states for all institutions under PDDC §12.4(c)(1).

(c) The Governance Secretariat cross-references the Constitutional Registry with the State Registry to maintain coherence between constitutional precedent and operational state records.


## PART 7 - Capture Tribunal Mechanics

### §7.1 - Panel Composition

(a) The Capture Tribunal (CT) operates as a standing three-Justice panel designated by the full Court.

(b) Designations rotate on two-year schedules to prevent capture of the Tribunal itself.

(c) Any Justice with a disclosed conflict in a matter before the CT is replaced by an alternate Justice designated by the full Court.

### §7.2 - Capture Proceeding Intake

(a) Capture proceedings may be initiated by: any Full Avian Member by petition; the Governance Secretariat upon detecting a State Registry anomaly; the CC upon observing conduct meeting the criteria of PDDC §13.1; or by the CoC acting sua sponte upon a pattern identified in the course of other proceedings.

(b) Petitions are acknowledged within forty-eight (48) hours.

(c) The CT determines within seven (7) days whether a prima facie capture pattern exists warranting full proceedings.

### §7.3 - Full Proceedings

(a) Upon finding prima facie evidence of capture or an FSM forbidden transition, the CT:

(1) Notifies the affected institution and individuals;

(2) Publishes the prima facie finding via FTS/OTF-1;

(3) Initiates the FSM fault handler under PDDC §12.4 for the affected domain; and

(4) Convenes a full evidentiary hearing within fourteen (14) days.

(b) All parties are entitled to be heard, to present evidence, and to be represented.

(c) The CT issues a final decision within thirty (30) days of the evidentiary hearing.

### §7.4 - FSM Fault Attribution

(a) FSM fault attribution determines which individuals or institutions are responsible for an attempted forbidden transition.

(b) Fault attribution findings are separate from and follow the automatic fault handler execution under PDDC §12.4.

(c) The fault handler protocol is self-executing and does not await Tribunal attribution findings.


# TITLE 4 - TREASURY AND ISRU MECHANICS

## PART 8 - Treasury Assembly Operations

### §8.1 - Meeting Schedule

(a) The Treasury Assembly (TA) meets in ordinary session monthly.

(b) The TA must hold a Budget Preparation Session beginning no later than three (3) months before the end of the fiscal year.

(c) Extraordinary sessions may be called by the TA Chair or any three (3) TA Members.

(d) All TA sessions are published via FTS/OTF-1 within seventy-two (72) hours.

### §8.2 - Budget Process

(a) The TA prepares the draft Annual Budget beginning in Month 10 of the fiscal year.

(b) The draft Budget is presented to the CC for review and comment in Month 11.

(c) The final draft Budget, incorporating CC comments, is submitted to the GA for approval no later than the first day of Month 12.

(d) The GA must vote on the Annual Budget no later than the last day of the fiscal year.

(e) If the GA does not vote by the deadline, the prior year's budget continues in force on a month-by-month basis until a new budget is adopted, with no expenditure increases permitted.

### §8.3 - ISRU License Administration

(a) ISRU license applications are submitted to the TA Secretariat, which reviews for completeness within fourteen (14) days.

(b) The TA votes on license approval within thirty (30) days of a complete application.

(c) License decisions are published via FTS/OTF-1 within forty-eight (48) hours of the vote.

(d) License holders file quarterly compliance reports covering royalty remittances, labor standards compliance, and environmental obligations.

(e) The Independent Fiscal Auditor reviews compliance reports annually and publishes findings.

### §8.4 - Protected Mode Financial Continuity Procedures

(a) During Protected Mode activation, the TA continues to perform all ordinary functions without modification.

(b) The TA Chair issues a Financial Continuity Notice within twenty-four (24) hours of Protected Mode activation, confirming:

(1) The current adopted budget remains in full force;

(2) All scheduled disbursements will proceed normally;

(3) The Protected Mode Continuity Reserve is accessible for life-sustaining disbursements if needed; and

(4) No budget reallocations will be made on the basis of Protected Mode status.

(c) The Financial Continuity Notice is a public document. It does not create new TA authority.

(d) Any TA action that redirects, suspends, or expands budget authority on the basis of Protected Mode status is an attempted forbidden transition under PDDC §12.3(a) and triggers the fault handler of PDDC §12.4.


## PART 9 - ISRU Economics and Revenue Management

### §9.1 - Royalty Collection

(a) ISRU royalty payments are made monthly by all license holders to the TA Secretariat.

(b) Royalties are calculated on gross extraction value as defined in the license agreement.

(c) Late payments incur interest at the Commonwealth standard rate.

(d) Persistent non-payment triggers license review proceedings.

### §9.2 - Commons Reserve Distribution

(a) The twenty percent (20%) Commons Reserve floor is calculated and set aside before any other distribution from each royalty payment.

(b) The Commons Reserve sub-accounts are:

(1) Life-Sustaining Infrastructure Fund — minimum forty percent (40%) of Commons Reserve;

(2) Universal Basic Provisions Fund — minimum twenty percent (20%) of Commons Reserve;

(3) RALC Obligations Fund — minimum fifteen percent (15%) of Commons Reserve;

(4) Long-Term Civilizational Investment Fund — minimum fifteen percent (15%) of Commons Reserve; and

(5) Protected Mode Continuity Reserve — minimum ten percent (10%) of Commons Reserve.

(c) The sub-account allocations may be adjusted by TA vote within the constraints above.

### §9.3 - Workers' Dividend

(a) A Workers' Dividend is distributed annually to all workers engaged in ISRU operations, regardless of membership category.

(b) The Workers' Dividend is calculated from the surplus remaining after all mandatory allocations.

(c) The Workers' Dividend Representative proposes the distribution formula annually, subject to TA approval.


# TITLE 5 - SECTOR AND WORKING GROUP MECHANICS

## PART 10 - Sector Governance Mechanics

### §10.1 - Sector Charter Adoption

(a) Sectors adopt their founding Charters through an internal ratification vote of founding Members with a minimum two-thirds (2/3) approval.

(b) The Charter is then submitted to the GA for formal recognition by two-thirds (2/3) GA resolution.

(c) The Charter is published via FTS/OTF-1 and registered in the State Registry upon GA recognition.

(d) The Sector's initial FSM state is Contributing upon Charter recognition.

### §10.2 - Sector Assembly Operations

(a) Each Sector Assembly (SA) meets in ordinary session at least quarterly.

(b) SA sessions follow democratic participation standards equivalent to those of the GA at the Sector level.

(c) SA decisions are published via FTS/OTF-1 within seventy-two (72) hours.

(d) Any SA decision that constitutes a forbidden transition under PDDC §12.3 is void and triggers the fault handler of PDDC §12.4.

### §10.3 - Sector FSM State Management

(a) Each Sector maintains an internal FSM State log updated on any state transition.

(b) The Sector's Presiding Officer notifies the Governance Secretariat of any state transition within twenty-four (24) hours.

(c) The Governance Secretariat updates the State Registry and publishes an alert via FTS/OTF-1.

(d) Sectors may not self-declare Protected Mode. Protected Mode is activated by the Constitutional Court fault handler under PDDC §12.4.

(e) A Sector that believes it is at risk of a forbidden transition may petition the Constitutional Court for a pre-emptive state review under PDDC §12.7(c).

### §10.4 - Sector-Level Protected Mode

(a) When a Sector's domain is the subject of a fault declaration under PDDC §12.4, the Sector enters Protected Mode automatically.

(b) During Sector-level Protected Mode:

(1) The Sector's governing Charter remains fully operative;

(2) Sector Members' rights are fully preserved;

(3) Sector-level governance bodies continue their ordinary functions;

(4) No Sector official may expand their authority on the basis of Protected Mode status; and

(5) The Sector reports to the Constitutional Court on Protected Mode operational status at least weekly.

(c) Sector-level Protected Mode is resolved by Constitutional Court certification per PDDC §12.5(d).

### §10.5 - Sector Avia — First Sector

(a) Sector Avia operates as the founding Sector of the Aevoric Commonwealth and is governed by the Sector Avia Charter ratified under the Keefeian Compact.

(b) Sector Avia's Presiding Officer holds an advisory seat at all GA Ordinary Sessions as the representative of the founding Sector.

(c) Sector Avia maintains the Aevoric cultural heritage archives, including the etymological and cultural record of Per Avia, Ad Astra and the Ascending Arc emblem.

### §10.6 - Sector Tier Classification

(a) Sectors are classified by the Treasury Assembly into Tier 1, Tier 2, or Tier 3 based on membership size, operational scale, and ISRU engagement.

(b) Tier classification affects Treasury Assembly delegate representation and resource allocation ratios.

(c) Tier classification is reviewed annually and may be appealed by a Sector to the GA.

### §10.7 - Inter-Sector Coordination

(a) Sectors may establish inter-Sector Working Groups for coordinated initiatives.

(b) Inter-Sector Working Groups operate under the authority of the participating Sector Assemblies jointly.

(c) Inter-Sector Working Groups are listed in the State Registry as sub-entities of the participating Sectors.


## PART 11 - Working Group and Guild Mechanics

### §11.1 - Working Group Establishment

(a) Working Groups are established by CC resolution or GA resolution specifying: the Working Group's mandate; its composition; its reporting relationship; and its dissolution condition or date.

(b) Working Groups are listed in the State Registry upon establishment and removed upon dissolution.

(c) Working Groups operate in the Contributing state by default.

### §11.2 - Working Group Reporting

(a) Working Groups submit progress reports to their establishing body at least monthly.

(b) Working Group final reports are published via FTS/OTF-1 within thirty (30) days of mandate completion.

(c) A Working Group that has not reported for sixty (60) days is flagged in the State Registry as Resting, and the CC is notified.

### §11.3 - Guild Mechanics

(a) Guilds operate as standing bodies with continuous membership and governance obligations.

(b) Guild governance must satisfy the democratic participation standards of PDDC §2.1 through §2.6 at the Guild level.

(c) The Guild of Workers' Standards elects the Workers' Dividend Representative to the Treasury Assembly in accordance with PDDC Treasury and Funding instrument requirements.

(d) Guilds that wish to pursue Sector elevation submit an elevation petition to the GA with evidence of sustained membership, governance, and operational activity.


# TITLE 6 - FSM STATE MONITORING MECHANICS

## PART 12 - FSM State Monitoring Procedures

### §12.1 - State Registry Maintenance Procedures

(a) The Governance Secretariat maintains the State Registry as the live, continuously updated record of all Commonwealth institutions' operational states, per PDDC §12.7.

(b) The State Registry is maintained as a structured database with the following required fields for each entry:

(1) Institution name and identifier;

(2) Current valid FSM state under PDDC §12.2;

(3) Date and time of last state transition;

(4) Reason for last state transition (brief, plain-language description);

(5) Responsible official confirming the transition;

(6) Constitutional Registry reference (last known safe state version); and

(7) Any active Protected Mode status and activation date.

(c) The State Registry is published via FTS/OTF-1 in real time. Any delay exceeding one (1) hour in publishing a known state transition is itself a State Registry anomaly subject to §12.2 investigation.

(d) The Governance Secretariat performs a full State Registry reconciliation audit quarterly, comparing registry records to documented governance actions and identifying any undocumented state transitions.

(e) The quarterly reconciliation audit report is published via FTS/OTF-1 and submitted to the Constitutional Court and the Independent Fiscal Auditor.

### §12.2 - Fault Detection and Identification

(a) A fault is the detection of an actual or attempted transition toward a forbidden state under PDDC §12.3. Faults may be detected by:

(1) Automated monitoring systems integrated with the State Registry that flag proposals, directives, or legislative instruments containing provisions consistent with forbidden transitions;

(2) The Governance Secretariat during routine review of CC Coordinating Directives, GA proposals, or Sector Charter submissions;

(3) Any Full Avian Member by petition to the Constitutional Court under PDDC §12.7(c);

(4) The Constitutional Court acting sua sponte during any proceeding; or

(5) The Capture Tribunal in the course of capture proceedings under PDDC TITLE 6.

(b) Fault detection does not require confirmation of a completed forbidden transition. Detection of a trajectory, pattern, or instrument whose natural or intended operation would produce a forbidden transition is sufficient to trigger the fault handler.

(c) Upon fault detection, the detecting party — whether automated system, Secretariat officer, Member, or Court — immediately logs the detection in the State Registry with the time, the detecting party, the instrument or behavior identified, and the forbidden transition category under PDDC §12.3(a) toward which it tends.

(d) No delay is permitted between fault detection and State Registry logging. A detector who knowingly delays logging a detected fault is subject to Capture Tribunal review.

### §12.3 - Fault Declaration Procedures

(a) Fault declaration is the formal architectural rejection of an offending instrument under PDDC §12.4(b)(1). It is the first step of the fault handler protocol.

(b) Fault declaration is triggered automatically upon confirmed fault detection. It does not require a vote, a hearing, a decision by any official, or any authorization beyond the detection itself.

(c) Upon fault declaration:

(1) The offending instrument is immediately suspended. No action may proceed under the suspended instrument pending Constitutional Court resolution.

(2) The Constitutional Court Registrar is notified by automated alert within one (1) minute of declaration.

(3) The fault is logged to FTS/OTF-1 within fifteen (15) minutes of declaration.

(4) The Constitutional Court convenes its FSM panel within forty-eight (48) hours.

(d) The Governance Secretariat publishes a Fault Declaration Notice via FTS/OTF-1 within two (2) hours of declaration, containing:

(1) The identifier of the offending instrument;

(2) The forbidden transition category (PDDC §12.3(a)(1), (2), (3), or (4)) toward which it tends;

(3) The domain affected;

(4) The timestamp of declaration; and

(5) The status of automatic reversion and Protected Mode activation.

(e) The Fault Declaration Notice is a public document. It is not a political statement. It is an architectural record.

### §12.4 - Protected Mode Activation Procedures

(a) Protected Mode activates automatically upon fault declaration under §12.3. It requires no vote, no authorization, and no executive action.

(b) The Governance Secretariat publishes the Protected Mode Status Record via FTS/OTF-1 within one (1) hour of activation, containing:

(1) The institution or domain in Protected Mode;

(2) The fault that triggered activation;

(3) The reverted configuration under PDDC §12.4(c);

(4) The list of core functions confirmed as operational;

(5) The rights status confirmation (all PDDC TITLE 4 rights fully operative); and

(6) The scheduled Constitutional Court review date (within thirty (30) days).

(c) During Protected Mode, the Governance Secretariat provides the Constitutional Court with weekly Protected Mode Status Updates confirming that:

(1) Core functions remain operational;

(2) No authority has been expanded;

(3) All rights remain fully operative; and

(4) The State Registry accurately reflects all current operational states.

(d) The Constitutional Court may request additional Protected Mode Status Updates at any time.

(e) Protected Mode is displayed prominently in the State Registry and Implementation Dashboard for the duration of activation.

### §12.5 - State Recovery and Registry Certification

(a) Protected Mode is resolved when the Constitutional Court certifies under PDDC §12.5(d) that:

(1) The offending instrument has been remediated or nullified;

(2) The last known safe state has been verified as rights-compliant; and

(3) No further forbidden transition risk from the same source exists.

(b) The Constitutional Court issues a State Recovery Certificate, which is a public document published via FTS/OTF-1.

(c) Upon issuance of the State Recovery Certificate, the Governance Secretariat:

(1) Updates the State Registry to reflect the institution's return to its recovered valid operational state;

(2) Closes the Protected Mode Status Record;

(3) Archives the complete fault event record — declaration, reversion, Protected Mode period, and recovery — in the Constitutional Registry as a permanent historical record; and

(4) Publishes a State Registry Recovery Notice via FTS/OTF-1 within two (2) hours.

(d) The archived fault event record is a permanent public record. It may not be expunged, modified, or reclassified.

(e) The Governance Secretariat updates the Implementation Dashboard to reflect the resolved fault and any milestone tolling adjustments per PDDC-IMPLEMENTATION-ROADMAP §8.3(b).

### §12.6 - Annual FSM Monitoring Report

(a) The Governance Secretariat, in coordination with the Constitutional Court and the Capture Tribunal, publishes an Annual FSM Monitoring Report each Year A.E.

(b) The Annual FSM Monitoring Report covers:

(1) Total number of state transitions recorded in the State Registry;

(2) Total number of fault detections and declarations;

(3) Duration and domain of each Protected Mode activation;

(4) Outcome of each fault resolution;

(5) Capture Tribunal proceedings initiated and resolved;

(6) State Registry audit findings from the four quarterly reconciliations; and

(7) Recommendations for improving detection, declaration, and recovery procedures.

(c) The Annual FSM Monitoring Report is published via FTS/OTF-1 and presented to the GA at the first Ordinary Session of each new Year A.E.

(d) The GA may direct the CC to implement recommendations from the Annual FSM Monitoring Report by Coordinating Directive.
