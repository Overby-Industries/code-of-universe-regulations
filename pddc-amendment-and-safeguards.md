# TITLE 0 - CONSTITUTIONAL AMENDMENT PDDC AMENDMENT AND SAFEGUARDS

## PART 0 - The Aevoric Commonwealth - Code of Universe Regulations (CUR)

### §0.1 - Governing Instrument - Constitutional Court and General Assembly Authority - Effective:
 
Day 1, Year 1 A.E. (2026-05-20 C.E.)

### §0.2 - PREAMBLE

This instrument establishes the complete procedures for amending the Progressive Direct Democracy Constitution of the Aevoric Commonwealth and the full body of anti-capture safeguards that protect the Commonwealth from institutional subversion. These two domains - the power to change fundamental law, and the power to resist its corruption - are inseparable. An amendment process without anti-capture safeguards is an invitation to constitutional manipulation. Anti-capture safeguards without a legitimate amendment process are an invitation to rigid authoritarianism. This instrument holds them in balance.

Every provision of this instrument is read against the foundational commitment of the PDDC to non-domination as defined in §2.5 of the Constitution, radical transparency as defined in §2.4, and the Rights for All Life. The Constitutional Court is the primary guardian of this instrument; its independence is itself an anti-capture requirement and an FSM invariant. All provisions referencing governance disruption are governed exclusively by the FSM Fault Handler Protocol as defined in §12.4 of the Constitution and elaborated herein. The Aevoric Commonwealth does not recognize emergency powers in any form. Per Avia, Ad Astra.


# TITLE 1 - CONSTITUTIONAL AMENDMENT

## PART 1 - Amendment Philosophy

### §1.1 - Living Constitution Principle

The PDDC is designed to evolve. Governance structures, economic realities, and the understanding of rights all change over time. The amendment process exists to channel that evolution through legitimate, deliberative, democratically accountable procedures - not to prevent change, but to ensure that change reflects genuine collective will rather than the preferences of any faction, coalition, or external interest.

### §1.2 - Stability and Adaptability

Amendment procedures are calibrated to the significance of the provision being changed. Routine governance adjustments flow through ordinary GA legislation. Core structural and rights provisions require supermajority thresholds and in some cases full membership referenda. Entrenched provisions require a Constitutional Convention, which is the highest and most deliberative democratic process available to the Aevoric Commonwealth. This calibration reflects the principle that the more fundamental the provision, the wider and deeper the consent required to alter it.

### §1.3 - Anti-Regressive Constraint

No amendment may reduce or eliminate a right guaranteed by the Rights for All Life Charter. No amendment may lower the democratic participation thresholds required for constitutional amendments of any category. No amendment may grant any individual, faction, Sector, or external entity disproportionate control over any governance institution of the Aevoric Commonwealth. No amendment may alter, weaken, or work around the FSM Governance Architecture established in TITLE 5 of the Constitution except through a Type A Constitutional Convention process. Proposed amendments violating these constraints are void ab initio and shall be enjoined by the Constitutional Court before ratification.

### §1.4 - FSM Compliance as Amendment Prerequisite

Every proposed amendment, regardless of category, must include a declaration of FSM Compliance affirming that the proposed text does not:

(a) Create, invoke, or functionally approximate emergency powers in any form;

(b) Render any forbidden transition architecturally reachable as defined in §12.3 of the Constitution;

(c) Interfere with the automatic operation of the Fault Handler Protocol as defined in §12.4 of the Constitution;

(d) Expand the authority of any institution or official during Protected Mode as prohibited by §12.5 and §12.6 of the Constitution; or

(e) Eliminate or reduce the rights held by any Recognized Life-Form as FSM invariants under §10.1 of the Constitution.

Proposals failing FSM Compliance review are returned to the proposer without prejudice to re-submission after remediation.


## PART 2 - Amendment Categories

### §2.1 - Category Classification

All constitutional provisions are classified into one of four amendment categories based on their structural significance to the Aevoric Commonwealth:

(a) Type A - Entrenched provisions are those whose alteration would fundamentally change the nature of the Commonwealth's constitutional order. Type A provisions include:

(1) §2.1 (Direct Democracy Commitment);

(2) §2.5 (Non-Domination Principle);

(3) §9.1 (Common Heritage of Resources);

(4) §10.1(a), (c), (d), (f), and (i) (Non-Derogable Rights including the right to a valid FSM state);

(5) §12.3 (Forbidden Transitions - architecturally unreachable states);

(6) §12.6 (Emergency Powers Prohibition); and

(7) §13.1 (Capture Prohibition).

Amendment of any Type A provision requires a Constitutional Convention followed by a full membership referendum with fifty percent (50%) participation and three-quarters (3/4) approval.

(b) Type B - Foundational provisions establish the primary institutional architecture and the FSM governance framework of the Commonwealth. Type B provisions include:

(1) TITLE 1 (Foundational Principles);

(2) TITLE 2 (Governance Institutions);

(3) TITLE 5 (FSM Governance Architecture); and

(4) TITLE 6 (Anti-Capture Framework).

Amendment of any Type B provision requires a three-quarters (3/4) GA supermajority with fifty percent (50%) quorum plus a direct membership referendum with forty percent (40%) participation and three-quarters (3/4) approval.

(c) Type C - Structural provisions govern specific domains of Commonwealth operations. Type C provisions include:

(1) TITLE 3 (ISRU Economy);

(2) TITLE 4 (Rights Floor);

(3) TITLE 8 (CUR and RALC Integration); and

(4) TITLE 9 (Ratification and Continuity).

Amendment of any Type C provision requires a three-quarters (3/4) GA supermajority with fifty percent (50%) quorum and sixty (60) day publication, with no additional referendum unless twenty percent (20%) of Full Members petition for one within the publication period.

(d) Type D - Operational provisions are implementation details and procedural rules that do not alter core structures, rights floors, or institutional architecture. Amendment of Type D provisions requires a two-thirds (2/3) GA supermajority with forty percent (40%) quorum and thirty (30) day publication.

### §2.2 - Classification Disputes

If there is a dispute over which category applies to a proposed amendment, the Constitutional Court determines the classification before the amendment process proceeds. Any Member may petition the Court of Conscience for a classification ruling within ten (10) days of a proposal's publication. The Constitutional Court shall issue its classification determination within twenty-one (21) days of receiving the petition. Classification determinations are binding on all governance bodies and may not be circumvented by re-labeling or re-framing the proposal.

### §2.3 - FSM Architecture Protected from Reclassification

No provision of TITLE 5 of the Constitution (FSM Governance Architecture) may be reclassified to a lower amendment category by any court ruling, GA resolution, or CC action. Any attempt to reclassify FSM Architecture provisions constitutes an attempted forbidden transition as defined in §12.3 of the Constitution and triggers the Fault Handler Protocol under §12.4.


## PART 3 - Proposal Process

### §3.1 - Methods of Proposal

A constitutional amendment may be proposed by any of the following methods:

(a) General Assembly vote to initiate, by two-thirds (2/3) majority of those voting with forty percent (40%) quorum;

(b) Coordinating Council unanimous resolution submitted to the GA for initiation;

(c) Member petition with validated signatures of ten percent (10%) of Full Members, submitted to the Election Commission; or

(d) Constitutional Convention resolution by majority of Convention delegates, where a Convention has been convened under §5.1 of this Title.

### §3.2 - Petition Validation

Member Initiative petitions are submitted to the Election Commission, which validates signatures within fourteen (14) days. Defective signatures may be cured within seven (7) additional days. A valid petition automatically places the amendment on the next GA agenda as a priority item. The Election Commission shall publish all petition outcomes including the number of valid and invalid signatures, the reason for any invalidity finding, and the cure process used.

### §3.3 - Proposal Requirements

Every amendment proposal must include:

(a) The exact text proposed, showing additions, deletions, and modifications clearly in tracked-changes format;

(b) A Statement of Purpose explaining the need for the amendment and its expected effect on the Commonwealth and all Recognized Life-Forms;

(c) A RALC Compatibility Statement certifying that the amendment does not diminish rights guaranteed by the Rights for All Life Charter, signed by the RALC Finance Liaison or RALC Standing Board representative;

(d) An FSM Compliance Declaration as required by §1.4 of this Title, prepared by the proposer and independently reviewed by the Anti-Capture Compliance Officer;

(e) An Anti-Regression Analysis confirming the amendment does not violate §1.3 of this Title; and

(f) A Fiscal Impact Assessment if the amendment affects resource allocation, treasury obligations, or ISRU revenue distribution.

### §3.4 - Court of Conscience Pre-Review

For all Type A and Type B proposed amendments, the Court of Conscience shall conduct a pre-review within thirty (30) days of proposal publication. The pre-review assesses:

(a) Whether the proposal violates §1.3 (Anti-Regressive Constraint);

(b) Whether the FSM Compliance Declaration is complete and substantively accurate;

(c) Whether the proposal contains provisions that constitute or approximate emergency powers in any form; and

(d) Whether the classification assigned by the proposer is correct under §2.1.

Pre-review findings are advisory but must be addressed in a written response by the proposer before the amendment may proceed to the deliberation phase. If the CoC finds that a proposal violates the Anti-Regressive Constraint or contains emergency power provisions, the proposal is suspended pending remediation.


## PART 4 - Deliberation Requirements

### §4.1 - Publication Periods

All proposed amendments shall be published to the Full Transparency Stack (FTS/OTF-1) for the following minimum periods before any ratification vote:

(a) Type A amendments: ninety (90) days from proposal publication;

(b) Type B amendments: sixty (60) days from proposal publication;

(c) Type C amendments: sixty (60) days from proposal publication; and

(d) Type D amendments: thirty (30) days from proposal publication.

Publication periods may not be shortened by any vote, resolution, or court order. Any attempt to shorten a publication period constitutes a fault under the FSM Fault Handler Protocol.

### §4.2 - Civic Education Requirement

For Type A and Type B amendments, the Governance Secretariat shall conduct at least three (3) public education sessions during the publication period, open to all Members and accessible to all Recognized Life-Forms with means to participate. Education sessions shall explain the proposed amendment, its effects, and the ratification process without advocating for or against the proposal. Session recordings shall be published to FTS/OTF-1 within forty-eight (48) hours.

### §4.3 - General Assembly Debate Minimums

Before any amendment ratification vote in the GA, the following minimum debate time must be observed:

(a) Type A: not less than three (3) full GA sessions dedicated to the amendment;

(b) Type B: not less than two (2) full GA sessions dedicated to the amendment;

(c) Type C: not less than one (1) full GA session with the amendment as a primary agenda item; and

(d) Type D: not less than one (1) dedicated GA debate segment.

Debate minimums may not be waived by unanimous consent or any procedural motion.

### §4.4 - Right of Response

During the publication period, any Member, Sector Assembly, or Recognized Life-Form may submit a formal Response to a proposed amendment. Responses are published alongside the proposal on FTS/OTF-1 and must be made available to all Members before any ratification vote.


## PART 5 - Constitutional Convention

### §5.1 - Convening Authority

A Constitutional Convention is required for all Type A amendments. A Constitutional Convention may also be convened:

(a) By petition of twenty percent (20%) of Full Members, validated by the Election Commission; or

(b) By a two-thirds (2/3) GA vote, provided no Type A amendment process is already underway.

### §5.2 - Delegate Composition

A Constitutional Convention shall be composed of delegates selected as follows:

(a) Forty percent (40%) elected delegates, chosen by Full Members via Ranked Choice Voting, one delegate per two hundred (200) Full Members or fraction thereof, with a minimum of ten (10) delegates;

(b) Forty percent (40%) sortition delegates, selected by verified random lottery from a pool of Full Members who have not held elected Commonwealth office within the preceding two (2) years;

(c) Ten percent (10%) Recognized Life-Form delegates, selected by the RALC Standing Board from among Recognized Life-Forms who are not Full Members; and

(d) Ten percent (10%) underrepresented group delegates, selected by the Court of Conscience from Members who belong to historically underrepresented groups within the Commonwealth as identified in the most recent Governance Health Index.

### §5.3 - Convention Governance

The Convention shall elect its own Chair and Secretariat by majority vote of delegates at its first session. The Convention sets its own rules of procedure by majority vote, subject to the following non-waivable requirements:

(a) All sessions shall be open to public observation;

(b) All votes shall be recorded and published on FTS/OTF-1 within twenty-four (24) hours;

(c) No proposal may be voted on until it has been before the Convention for at least seven (7) days;

(d) Any delegate may call for a written record of dissent, which shall be published alongside any Convention resolution; and

(e) The Convention shall produce an FSM Compliance Report for each proposed amendment before final vote.

### §5.4 - Scope Limitation

A Constitutional Convention is limited to the specific amendment or amendments that triggered its convening. The Convention may not propose amendments beyond its stated scope without a new petition or GA authorization. Scope disputes are resolved by the Constitutional Court within seven (7) days of referral.

### §5.5 - Duration

A Constitutional Convention shall complete its work within ninety (90) days of its first session. If the Convention cannot complete its work within ninety (90) days, it may request a single extension of up to thirty (30) days by two-thirds (2/3) vote of delegates. If work is not completed within the extended period, the Convention is dissolved and the amendment process must begin again.

### §5.6 - Convention FSM Compliance

The Constitutional Convention is itself subject to the FSM Governance Architecture. No Convention action may render a forbidden transition reachable, invoke emergency powers, or expand governance authority beyond the Convention's defined scope. Any Convention resolution that violates these constraints is void and triggers the Fault Handler Protocol under §12.4 of the Constitution.


## PART 6 - Ratification Process

### §6.1 - Type A Ratification

A Type A amendment proposed by a Constitutional Convention is ratified by:

(a) Convention approval by two-thirds (2/3) of all delegates;

(b) Publication of the approved text for thirty (30) days following Convention approval; and

(c) A full membership referendum with fifty percent (50%) participation threshold and three-quarters (3/4) approval of those voting.

If the participation threshold is not met in the first referendum, a second referendum shall be held not less than sixty (60) days later. If the participation threshold is again not met, the amendment fails and may not be re-proposed for two (2) years.

### §6.2 - Type B Ratification

A Type B amendment is ratified by:

(a) GA approval by three-quarters (3/4) of those voting with fifty percent (50%) quorum; and

(b) A direct membership referendum with forty percent (40%) participation threshold and three-quarters (3/4) approval of those voting.

### §6.3 - Type C Ratification

A Type C amendment is ratified by GA approval by three-quarters (3/4) of those voting with fifty percent (50%) quorum following the sixty (60) day publication period. If a petition of twenty percent (20%) of Full Members requests a referendum, the referendum shall be held before ratification is complete, requiring simple majority approval with thirty percent (30%) participation.

### §6.4 - Type D Ratification

A Type D amendment is ratified by GA approval by two-thirds (2/3) of those voting with forty percent (40%) quorum following the thirty (30) day publication period.

### §6.5 - Referendum Administration

All membership referenda under this Part are administered by the Election Commission using the same security, accessibility, and transparency standards as GA elections. Results are published in full on FTS/OTF-1 within twenty-four (24) hours of vote close. The Election Commission shall certify results within seventy-two (72) hours.

### §6.6 - Effective Date

Ratified amendments take effect fourteen (14) days after certification by the Election Commission, unless the amendment specifies a later effective date. No amendment may take effect retroactively or before certification.


## PART 7 - Anti-Capture in the Amendment Process

### §7.1 - Pre-Ratification Capture Screening

Before any Type A or Type B amendment may proceed to ratification, the Anti-Capture Compliance Officer shall conduct a Capture Screening Assessment examining:

(a) Whether any entity holding more than five percent (5%) of ISRU license concentration has materially supported the amendment's proposal;

(b) Whether any CC Member, Constitutional Court Justice, or senior official has an undisclosed financial interest in the amendment's outcome;

(c) Whether the amendment proposal process involved coordination with external actors not disclosed in the proposal's Statement of Purpose; and

(d) Whether the amendment, if ratified, would benefit a narrow class of actors disproportionate to its stated public purpose.

The ACCO shall publish Capture Screening findings on FTS/OTF-1 no fewer than fourteen (14) days before any ratification vote.

### §7.2 - Prohibition on Capture-Motivated Amendments

No amendment may be proposed, advanced, or ratified if its primary effect is to:

(a) Exempt a specific entity, individual, or class from accountability mechanisms;

(b) Concentrate governance authority in fewer hands than the pre-amendment state;

(c) Reduce disclosure requirements or transparency obligations; or

(d) Weaken the FSM Governance Architecture, Anti-Capture Framework, or the rights of Recognized Life-Forms.

The Constitutional Court may void any amendment found to be capture-motivated before or after ratification. An amendment voided after ratification reverts the Constitution to its pre-amendment text.

### §7.3 - Institutional Reconstitution Following Capture

If the Constitutional Court finds that the amendment process itself was captured:

(a) The resulting amendment is void ab initio;

(b) The Fault Handler Protocol under §12.4 of the Constitution is triggered;

(c) The Anti-Capture Compliance Officer initiates a full institutional audit; and

(d) The GA must convene within thirty (30) days to consider reconstitution of any institution whose process was compromised.

Reconstitution does not require a new amendment. It is a fault recovery action, not a structural change.


# TITLE 2 - ANTI-CAPTURE SAFEGUARDS

## PART 8 - Foundational Anti-Capture Architecture

### §8.1 - Capture as Forbidden Transition

Institutional capture of the Aevoric Commonwealth's governance institutions is not merely a policy failure. It is an FSM forbidden transition as defined in §12.3 of the Constitution. Capture attempts trigger the Fault Handler Protocol automatically and without requirement of prior political action. No legislative, executive, or judicial action is required to activate fault handler mechanisms - they are self-executing at the architectural level per CUR-FOUNDATION-001.

### §8.2 - Capture Defined

For purposes of this instrument, institutional capture means any condition in which:

(a) A governance institution systematically favors the interests of a specific individual, faction, external entity, or narrow class over its constitutional mandate;

(b) Decision-making authority has been concentrated in fewer actors than the constitutional architecture requires, whether by formal action, informal practice, or gradual norm erosion;

(c) Accountability mechanisms are selectively applied or systematically evaded by those who hold governance authority; or

(d) Any forbidden transition as defined in §12.3 of the Constitution has been achieved, attempted, or architecturally enabled.

### §8.3 - Capture Typology

The following categories of capture are recognized for detection, reporting, and enforcement purposes:

(a) Financial Capture - ISRU license holders, treasury contractors, or economic actors exercise disproportionate influence over budget, fiscal policy, or resource allocation decisions;

(b) Electoral Capture - actors manipulate candidate pools, election administration, or campaign resources to predetermine electoral outcomes;

(c) Regulatory Capture - Sector or Working Group governance is redirected toward the interests of the entities it is intended to regulate;

(d) Informational Capture - control of the FTS/OTF-1 transparency infrastructure, official communications, or public information channels is exercised to shape narrative in favor of a particular actor;

(e) Proxy Capture - capture is achieved indirectly through shell entities, contractor relationships, sub-charters, or intermediary organizations not subject to direct PDDC accountability;

(f) Network Capture - governance influence is concentrated through informal coordination, relationship networks, or social pressure outside formal procedures;

(g) AI-Mediated Capture - automated systems, algorithmic tools, or artificial intelligence are deployed to concentrate decision influence, circumvent deliberation, or create asymmetric information advantages; and

(h) External Capture - actors outside the Aevoric Commonwealth exercise governance influence through treaty commitments, economic leverage, or informal relationships with Commonwealth officials.

### §8.4 - FSM Detection Mapping

Each capture type under §8.3 is mapped to the following FSM detection signals:

(a) Financial Capture: Treasury Assembly vote pattern divergence from GA mandate by more than fifteen percent (15%) over two consecutive fiscal years;

(b) Electoral Capture: Election Commission independence score below threshold as measured in the Governance Health Index;

(c) Regulatory Capture: Sector audit finding that compliance enforcement actions disproportionately target non-affiliated entities at a ratio exceeding three to one;

(d) Informational Capture: FTS/OTF-1 publication delays exceeding protocol standards by more than forty-eight (48) hours on more than three (3) occasions within ninety (90) days;

(e) Proxy Capture: undisclosed affiliated entity relationships identified in ACCO quarterly review;

(f) Network Capture: quorum-achieving voting blocs with greater than eighty percent (80%) vote correlation over three (3) consecutive GA sessions;

(g) AI-Mediated Capture: automated decision influence identified by ACCO technical audit; and

(h) External Capture: undisclosed external communications by CC Members identified by ACCO investigation.

### §8.5 - Capture Severity Classification

Capture incidents and indicators are classified by severity as follows:

(a) Category 1 - Procedural: minor deviation from constitutional procedure without systematic pattern; remedy is Compliance Order;

(b) Category 2 - Structural: pattern of procedural deviation indicating systemic drift; remedy is Structural Compliance Plan with CC and GA oversight;

(c) Category 3 - Institutional: documented bias of governance institution toward non-public interest; remedy is Institutional Remediation with Constitutional Court supervision; and

(d) Category 4 - Constitutional: documented forbidden transition attempt or achievement; remedy is Fault Handler Protocol activation, Immediate Fault Intervention Order, and Capture Tribunal proceedings.


## PART 9 - Structural Firewalls

### §9.1 - Financial Separation

The Treasury Assembly shall not be subject to direction, instruction, or informal pressure from the Coordinating Council on any budgetary matter. No CC Member may hold a simultaneous TA voting seat. No entity holding more than ten percent (10%) of ISRU license concentration may place representatives on the Treasury Assembly through any mechanism. These requirements are FSM invariants; their dismantling constitutes an attempted forbidden transition.

### §9.2 - Electoral Independence

The Election Commission is a fully independent body accountable to the Constitutional Court and the General Assembly. No CC Member, TA Member, or Sector executive may instruct, direct, or pressure the Election Commission on election administration matters. Election Commission decisions are final for election administration purposes and subject only to judicial review by the Constitutional Court.

### §9.3 - Judicial Independence

Constitutional Court Justices serve fixed terms and may be removed only through the impeachment procedures defined in the PDDC. No Justice may be assigned to a case involving an entity in which they hold a financial interest. Justices may not receive direction from the CC, TA, or any Sector on pending matters. Judicial independence is an FSM invariant.

### §9.4 - Anti-Capture Office Independence

The Anti-Capture Compliance Officer operates under the authority of the Court of Conscience and reports to the General Assembly, not the Coordinating Council. The ACCO has unrestricted access to all governance records, financial disclosures, and FTS/OTF-1 audit logs. No CC Member, TA Member, or official may instruct the ACCO to limit, redirect, or delay any investigation. Interference with ACCO operations constitutes a Category 4 capture incident.

### §9.5 - Information Integrity

The Full Transparency Stack (FTS/OTF-1) shall be administered by the Governance Secretariat under Constitutional Court oversight. No single entity may control publication access to FTS/OTF-1. Access credentials are distributed to the Constitutional Court, the ACCO, and the Election Commission as independent backup authorities. Tampering with FTS/OTF-1 records constitutes a fault under the Fault Handler Protocol and a Category 4 capture incident.

### §9.6 - Firewall Durability

No structural firewall under §9.1 through §9.5 may be dismantled, suspended, or reduced in scope by:

(a) CC resolution;

(b) TA vote;

(c) GA ordinary legislation; or

(d) Any Sector Charter provision.

Dismantling a structural firewall requires a Type A amendment process. Any attempt to dismantle a firewall by lesser means is void ab initio and triggers the Fault Handler Protocol.


## PART 10 - Mandatory Disclosure and Position Limits

### §10.1 - Mandatory Disclosure Requirements

The following disclosures are mandatory for all individuals holding elected or appointed governance positions in the Aevoric Commonwealth:

(a) All financial interests, business ownership stakes, and contractual relationships with entities that conduct business with the Commonwealth or hold ISRU licenses, updated annually and within fourteen (14) days of any material change;

(b) All external communications with non-Member entities regarding Commonwealth governance, policy, or resource allocation, disclosed within seventy-two (72) hours of occurrence;

(c) All gifts, services, travel, or other benefits received from entities with Commonwealth business interests, disclosed within forty-eight (48) hours of receipt; and

(d) All prior employment, advisory, or board roles with entities currently doing business with the Commonwealth, disclosed at the time of candidacy or appointment.

Disclosures are published on FTS/OTF-1 in full within forty-eight (48) hours of submission.

### §10.2 - Position Concentration Limits

No individual may simultaneously hold more than one of the following positions:

(a) CC executive role;

(b) TA voting seat;

(c) Constitutional Court seat;

(d) Court of Conscience seat;

(e) Election Commission seat; and

(f) Anti-Capture Compliance Officer appointment.

Holding two or more of these positions simultaneously is an automatic vacancy event in the most recently assumed position.

### §10.3 - ISRU License Concentration and Governance

No entity holding more than twenty-five percent (25%) of total ISRU license concentration may place individuals in CC, TA, or CoC positions through any mechanism. No entity holding more than ten percent (10%) of total ISRU license concentration may hold more than one seat across these three bodies. These limits are enforced by the Election Commission and the ACCO jointly.

### §10.4 - Cooling-Off Periods

The following cooling-off periods apply to officials leaving Commonwealth governance positions:

(a) CC Members: twenty-four (24) months before employment, advisory engagement, or board membership with any entity holding an ISRU license or government contract;

(b) Constitutional Court Justices: thirty-six (36) months before employment with any entity that had a matter before the Court during the Justice's service; and

(c) TA Members: eighteen (18) months before employment with any entity that received Commonwealth treasury funds during the Member's service.

Cooling-off violations are referred to the Constitutional Court and may result in disgorgement of compensation received during the violation period.


## PART 11 - Anti-Capture Compliance Officer

### §11.1 - Establishment and Authority

The Anti-Capture Compliance Officer (ACCO) is an independent officer of the Aevoric Commonwealth established to detect, investigate, and escalate capture incidents. The ACCO serves a four (4) year non-renewable term and is selected by the Court of Conscience through a competitive open process.

### §11.2 - Qualifications

The ACCO must demonstrate:

(a) No prior service in CC, TA, or Sector executive roles within the preceding five (5) years;

(b) No financial relationship with any ISRU license holder or Commonwealth contractor within the preceding three (3) years;

(c) Demonstrated expertise in governance systems, anti-corruption mechanisms, or institutional integrity; and

(d) Commitment to the FSM Governance Architecture as a condition of appointment.

### §11.3 - Independence Requirements

The ACCO:

(a) Reports quarterly to the General Assembly, not the Coordinating Council;

(b) Submits an annual Capture Risk Report to the Constitutional Court and GA simultaneously;

(c) May not be removed during their term except by a two-thirds (2/3) GA vote following Constitutional Court review; and

(d) Has unrestricted access to all governance records, financial disclosures, and FTS/OTF-1 audit logs without requiring CC authorization.

### §11.4 - Capture Risk Indicators

The ACCO shall monitor the following Capture Risk Indicators and initiate review when any threshold is crossed:

(a) Any single entity holds more than twenty percent (20%) of ISRU license concentration;

(b) CC vote correlation with a specific external interest group exceeds seventy percent (70%) over two consecutive quarters;

(c) GA attendance drops below thirty percent (30%) for two consecutive Ordinary Sessions;

(d) FTS/OTF-1 disclosure delays exceed seventy-two (72) hours on more than five (5) occasions within sixty (60) days;

(e) Whistleblower reports increase by more than fifty percent (50%) in a ninety-day period;

(f) Cooling-off period violations are reported for two or more officials within twelve (12) months; or

(g) The Governance Health Index score declines by more than ten (10) points in a single year.

### §11.5 - Quarterly Capture Risk Reports

The ACCO shall publish a Capture Risk Report within fifteen (15) days of each quarter-end to FTS/OTF-1. Each report shall include:

(a) Capture Risk Indicator status for each indicator under §11.4;

(b) Any investigations opened, pending, or closed during the quarter;

(c) Compliance Orders issued and their compliance status;

(d) Referrals made to the Capture Tribunal;

(e) FSM State monitoring findings relevant to capture detection; and

(f) A forward-looking risk assessment for the next quarter.


## PART 12 - Investigation and Enforcement

### §12.1 - Preliminary Investigation

Upon identifying a Capture Risk Indicator threshold crossing or receiving a credible complaint, the ACCO shall open a Preliminary Investigation. The investigation:

(a) Is non-public for the first thirty (30) days unless the ACCO determines public disclosure is required to prevent ongoing harm;

(b) May compel production of records, disclosures, and testimony from any Commonwealth official or entity doing business with the Commonwealth;

(c) Must be completed within sixty (60) days unless the ACCO certifies in writing that additional time is required and why; and

(d) Results in either a Compliance Order, a Capture Tribunal referral, an Immediate Fault Intervention Order request, or a finding of no actionable concern.

### §12.2 - Compliance Orders

A Compliance Order is issued for Category 1 and Category 2 capture incidents. Compliance Orders:

(a) State the specific conduct or condition constituting the capture indicator;

(b) Specify required remediation steps and a compliance deadline of not more than sixty (60) days;

(c) Are published on FTS/OTF-1 within forty-eight (48) hours of issuance; and

(d) Are monitored by the ACCO with a compliance verification report due within thirty (30) days of the compliance deadline.

Failure to comply with a Compliance Order within the specified deadline results in automatic referral to the Capture Tribunal.

### §12.3 - Capture Tribunal

The Capture Tribunal is a standing three-Justice panel of the Constitutional Court designated for anti-capture enforcement. The Capture Tribunal:

(a) Hears referrals from the ACCO for Category 2, 3, and 4 capture incidents;

(b) Has authority to compel testimony, production of records, and attendance of any Commonwealth official;

(c) May impose remedies including disgorgement, removal from office, structural reorganization of captured institutions, and referral to external legal authorities; and

(d) Publishes all proceedings, findings, and remedies on FTS/OTF-1 within seventy-two (72) hours of each proceeding.

### §12.4 - Immediate Fault Intervention Order

For Category 3 and Category 4 capture incidents where ongoing harm to Commonwealth governance integrity is probable, the ACCO may request an Immediate Fault Intervention Order (IFIO) from the Capture Tribunal. An IFIO:

(a) Is issued by the Capture Tribunal within twenty-four (24) hours of request;

(b) Immediately suspends the governance authority of named individuals pending full Tribunal proceedings;

(c) Is confirmed or modified by the Court of Conscience within forty-eight (48) hours of issuance;

(d) Does not expand the authority of any other individual or institution - suspended authority does not transfer, it is suspended;

(e) Triggers the FSM Fault Handler Protocol under §12.4 of the Constitution, reverting affected governance functions to the last known safe state configuration; and

(f) Is published on FTS/OTF-1 simultaneously with issuance, without exception.

An IFIO is a fault recovery mechanism. It is not an emergency power. No IFIO may be used to expand authority, suspend rights, or concentrate governance in fewer hands. Any IFIO that has the effect of expanding authority is void ab initio.

### §12.5 - Disgorgement Mechanics

The Capture Tribunal may order disgorgement of:

(a) Financial benefits received by an official during a period of capture or conflict of interest;

(b) Contracts, licenses, or approvals obtained through a captured governance process; and

(c) Governance advantages secured through conduct constituting capture.

Disgorgement amounts are deposited to the Commons Reserve under PDDC-TREASURY-AND-FUNDING §3.1 and may not be redirected by CC or TA action.

### §12.6 - Whistleblower Protection

Any Member, Worker, Recognized Life-Form, or contractor who reports a credible capture concern to the ACCO is entitled to:

(a) Anonymity at their election for the duration of the preliminary investigation;

(b) Legal protection against retaliation, including immediate referral of any retaliatory action to the Capture Tribunal;

(c) Reinstatement and compensation for any adverse employment action taken in connection with the report; and

(d) Recognition under the Governance Health Index's whistleblower participation metric.

Retaliation against a whistleblower is a Category 3 capture incident regardless of underlying circumstances.


## PART 13 - Bright Line Prohibitions

### §13.1 - Absolute Prohibitions

The following acts are void ab initio and constitute attempted forbidden transitions under §12.3 of the Constitution. They may not be authorized by any GA vote, CC resolution, TA action, Sector Charter, or Constitutional amendment of any category:

(a) Invoking, creating, or functionally approximating emergency powers under any name or framing;

(b) Suspending any right guaranteed under §10.1 of the Constitution for any reason including governance disruption, fault conditions, or Protected Mode activation;

(c) Expanding the authority of any institution or official during Protected Mode or any fault condition;

(d) Concentrating governance decision-making in fewer than the minimum number of actors required by the Constitution's quorum and majority thresholds;

(e) Directing the Anti-Capture Compliance Officer to limit, redirect, or delay an investigation;

(f) Tampering with FTS/OTF-1 records, disclosures, or publication schedules;

(g) Extending or indefinitely renewing any interim governance arrangement beyond the time limits specified in the Constitution or this instrument;

(h) Attempting to reclassify any Type A or FSM Architecture provision to a lower amendment category;

(i) Entering into any agreement with an external entity that transfers Commonwealth governance authority to that entity; and

(j) Enacting any provision that purports to make the Fault Handler Protocol optional, conditional, or subject to political override.

### §13.2 - Automatic Fault Activation

Any action constituting a Bright Line Prohibition under §13.1 automatically activates the FSM Fault Handler Protocol under §12.4 of the Constitution without requiring any additional governance action. The Governance Secretariat shall publish notice of Fault Handler activation on FTS/OTF-1 within one (1) hour of detection.


# TITLE 3 - GOVERNANCE HEALTH MONITORING

## PART 14 - Governance Health Index

### §14.1 - Establishment

The Governance Health Index (GHI) is a one-hundred point annual composite score measuring the overall health and capture-resistance of Aevoric Commonwealth governance institutions. The GHI is calculated annually by the Anti-Capture Compliance Officer in consultation with the Governance Secretariat and published to FTS/OTF-1 no later than sixty (60) days after the close of each fiscal year.

### §14.2 - GHI Scoring Dimensions

The GHI is composed of the following weighted dimensions:

(a) Participation (20 points): measures GA attendance, referendum participation, petition activity, and Sector Assembly engagement across all recognized membership categories;

(b) Transparency (20 points): measures FTS/OTF-1 publication timeliness, disclosure completeness, audit publication rates, and ACCO report accessibility;

(c) Equity (20 points): measures distribution of governance access, resource allocation alignment with rights floors, representation of Recognized Life-Forms across governance institutions, and Workers' Dividend distribution;

(d) Capture Resistance (25 points): measures ISRU license concentration, official cooling-off compliance, position concentration incidents, Capture Risk Indicator status, and Tribunal referral outcomes; and

(e) Constitutional Integrity (15 points): measures FSM State compliance across all institutions, amendment process adherence, CoC pre-review completion rates, and Constitutional Court case outcomes.

### §14.3 - GHI Thresholds and Response Requirements

GHI scores trigger the following required responses:

(a) Score of eighty (80) or above: Governance Health Status designated as Ascending - no mandatory response required beyond annual publication;

(b) Score of sixty-five (65) to seventy-nine (79): Governance Health Status designated as Caution - CC must publish a Governance Improvement Plan within thirty (30) days and report progress quarterly to the GA;

(c) Score of fifty (50) to sixty-four (64): Governance Health Status designated as Warning - GA must convene an Extraordinary Session within thirty (30) days; Constitutional Court review is mandatory; and CC must submit a Structural Remediation Plan within forty-five (45) days; and

(d) Score below fifty (50): Governance Health Status designated as Crisis - FSM Fault Handler Protocol is activated; Capture Tribunal convenes within seventy-two (72) hours; and a Type B amendment review of all affected governance domains is initiated automatically.

### §14.4 - Single-Year Decline Trigger

If the GHI declines by more than ten (10) points in a single year, the Court of Conscience must review the decline regardless of the absolute score. If the score remains at or above eighty (80) but has declined by more than ten (10) points, a Caution response is still required under §14.3(b).

### §14.5 - GHI Methodology Transparency

The full methodology, data sources, weighting calculations, and raw dimension scores underlying each annual GHI shall be published to FTS/OTF-1 alongside the summary score. Any Member may challenge the methodology before the Constitutional Court within sixty (60) days of publication.


## PART 15 - Periodic Constitutional Review

### §15.1 - Decennial Review

Every ten (10) years from Day 1, Year 1 A.E. (2026-05-20 C.E.), the Aevoric Commonwealth shall conduct a full Periodic Constitutional Review examining whether the Constitution and its associated instruments continue to serve their stated purposes and adequately protect all Recognized Life-Forms.

### §15.2 - Accelerated Review

A Periodic Constitutional Review may be initiated before the ten-year cycle by:

(a) GA vote by two-thirds (2/3) majority;

(b) Three (3) consecutive years of GHI scores below sixty-five (65); or

(c) Constitutional Court determination that structural drift has occurred across multiple governance domains.

### §15.3 - Review Process

A Periodic Constitutional Review shall:

(a) Be conducted by a Review Commission composed of equal thirds from the GA, the Constitutional Court, and a sortition panel of Full Members;

(b) Include public consultation sessions open to all Members and Recognized Life-Forms;

(c) Produce a published Review Report within one-hundred and eighty (180) days of commission formation; and

(d) Identify recommended amendments, if any, classified by amendment category under §2.1 of this Title.

Review Commission recommendations do not automatically initiate amendment proceedings. Any recommended amendment must be proposed through the standard process under §3.1.

### §15.4 - FSM Architecture Review

Each Periodic Constitutional Review shall include a dedicated examination of whether the FSM Governance Architecture under TITLE 5 of the Constitution continues to make all forbidden transitions architecturally unreachable. This examination is conducted by the ACCO in coordination with technical experts designated by the Constitutional Court and is published as a standalone appendix to the Review Report.


## PART 16 - Integration and Supremacy

### §16.1 - CUR-FOUNDATION-001 Authority

This instrument operates under and is consistent with CUR-FOUNDATION-001 (Anti-Capture and Resilience). Where any provision of this instrument appears to conflict with CUR-FOUNDATION-001, CUR-FOUNDATION-001 governs. No amendment to this instrument may reduce its conformity with CUR-FOUNDATION-001 without a simultaneous amendment to CUR-FOUNDATION-001 through that document's own amendment process.

### §16.2 - RALC Supremacy

In all matters of rights, this instrument operates under the Rights for All Life Charter. No provision of this instrument may be interpreted to reduce, limit, or condition any right guaranteed by the RALC.

### §16.3 - Constitution Cross-References

The following cross-references govern interpretation of this instrument:

(a) §12.3 of the Constitution defines all forbidden transitions referenced in this instrument;

(b) §12.4 of the Constitution defines the Fault Handler Protocol referenced in this instrument;

(c) §12.5 of the Constitution defines Protected Mode referenced in this instrument;

(d) §12.6 of the Constitution states the Emergency Powers Prohibition that this instrument reinforces; and

(e) §13.1 of the Constitution states the Capture Prohibition that this instrument enforces and operationalizes.

### §16.4 - Aevoric Identity

This instrument is a founding governance document of the Aevoric Commonwealth. It carries forward the founding commitment expressed in the motto Per Avia, Ad Astra - Through Flight, To the Stars. The Aevoric Commonwealth ascends by maintaining the integrity of its institutions, protecting the rights of all who live within its jurisdiction, and resisting the grounding forces of capture and corruption. This instrument is the Commonwealth's commitment to remain cleared for its constitutional flight path at all times.

### §16.5 - Effective Date and Ratification

This instrument takes effect on Day 1, Year 1 A.E. (2026-05-20 C.E.) as part of the initial PDDC ratification package. It may be amended only through the procedures it establishes, and no provision of this instrument may be suspended pending amendment.
