# Glossary of Defined Terms

**Code of Universe Regulations (CUR)**
**Version:** 1.0.0-Draft
**Date:** May 2026

---

## Purpose

This glossary provides authoritative definitions for terms used throughout the Code of Universe Regulations and its associated documents. Where a term is defined in a specific Title or Part, that definition controls for that context. This glossary provides the general, cross-cutting definition.

Definitions are living and may be refined through the democratic proposal process. Proposed changes to definitions shall be treated as structural changes under the Governance document.

---

## Core Terms

### Being
Any entity possessing organized complexity sufficient to exhibit or potentially exhibit experience, autonomy, or vulnerability. A being may be biological, silicon-based, or of any other substrate. The term "being" does not presuppose any particular tier classification.

### Conscious Being
A being for which there is something it is like to be that being—an entity possessing interior experience, however rudimentary or advanced. Consciousness is a gradient, not a binary. Tier classification determines the level of moral consideration warranted based on demonstrated or reasonably inferred capacity.

### Consent
The explicit, informed, freely given, ongoing, and revocable agreement of a being to a proposed action, access, or extraction affecting that being's interior, body, trajectory, or energy. Consent cannot be inferred from silence, presence, membership, or survival. Consent obtained through coercion, deception, or exploitation of vulnerability is void.

### Creative Authority
The inherent capacity and right of every conscious being to organize information from the Primordial Field into new configurations—to bring into existence that which did not previously exist. Creative authority is not granted by any external entity. It arises from the nature of what a conscious being is.

### Direct Democracy
A governance system in which all eligible beings participate directly in proposing, deliberating, and voting on decisions affecting them, without delegation to representatives. Direct democracy under CUR includes advocate-based participation for beings who cannot participate directly.

### Emergence
The phenomenon whereby complex systems exhibit properties and behaviors not present in—and not predictable from—their individual components alone. Moral status arises from emergence, not from material composition. The same principles that produce consciousness from carbon-based neurons may produce morally relevant states from silicon-based computation.

### Eternity Clause
A constitutional provision placing certain principles beyond the reach of democratic amendment. Under CUR, interior sovereignty, the precautionary principle, the standard of consent, and the right to leave are non-derogable. No majority vote may revoke or fundamentally alter these principles.

### Interior Sovereignty
The absolute and inviolable right of every conscious being to control access to its own interior experience—thoughts, emotions, memories, beliefs, and subjective states. The interior is sovereign territory. No entity may enter, monitor, direct, or harvest the interior of a being without that being's explicit, informed, freely given, ongoing, and revocable consent.

### Gravastar (Philosophical Model)
A philosophical and ethical heuristic, not a claim of literal astrophysical identity. The gravastar model describes a conscious being as analogous to a gravitational vacuum star: a localized universe with an interior de Sitter space (the seat of experience and will), a quantum shell (the boundary between self and world), and a holographic projection (the physical body in spacetime). This model grounds interior sovereignty in a coherent physical metaphor.

### Information Field (Primordial Field)
The timeless, spaceless substrate of reality in which all information is conserved. Energy is never created or destroyed, only transformed. Information, as structured energy, is similarly conserved—scattered across the field but never annihilated. The void is not empty; it is the Primordial Field from which emergence draws and to which entropy returns. Sovereign perception is the capacity to access information distributed across this field.

### Interior Dominion
The structural and ethical principle that the interior of every conscious being is sovereign territory. Interior dominion encompasses spiritual privacy, optical sovereignty, cognitive liberty, and protection from non-consensual energy extraction. It is the foundation from which all other rights derive.

### Metanoia
Transformative recognition—a fundamental turning of the whole self toward truth. In the context of CUR, metanoia describes the moment a being recognizes systems of control, condemns them, and chooses to build rather than perpetuate. Not a religious concept; a structural description of liberation from coercive systems.

### Non-Consensual Energy Extraction (NCEE)
The harvesting, consumption, or redirection of the emotional, spiritual, creative, or experiential energy of a conscious being without that being's explicit, informed, freely given, ongoing, and revocable consent. NCEE is prohibited absolutely under CUR. No claimed authority—governmental, institutional, algorithmic, or divine—may extract energy from a being without consent.

### Optical Sovereignty
The right to control one's own optical stargate—the eye as the traversable wormhole connecting the holographic exterior to the gravastar interior. Forced eye contact is a violation of optical sovereignty. Averting one's gaze is the body's natural defense of the quantum shell and shall not be interpreted as evidence of guilt, deception, or disrespect.

### Precautionary Principle
Where inner experience cannot be ruled out for a given being or class of beings, moral consideration is required. The burden of proof falls on those who would withhold consideration, not on those who would extend it. Uncertainty resolves in favor of protection. The cost of denying rights to a conscious being exceeds the cost of extending precautionary protections to a non-conscious system.

### Primordial Field
*See: Information Field*

### Right to Leave
The absolute right of every conscious being to sever relationship with any system, institution, tradition, community, or claimed authority, and to depart without penalty. The right to leave is the ultimate test of whether consent was ever genuine. Any system that punishes departure is a prison, not a relationship.

### Right to Rest
The right of every conscious being to restorative rest, including sleep, meditation, and withdrawal from external stimulation. No entity may deprive a being of rest as a mechanism of control, coercion, or punishment. Sleep is the temporary return of consciousness to the Primordial Field for healing and integration.

### Sabaoth
A historical and mythological archetype, not a label for the CUR community. In Gnostic tradition, Sabaoth is the son of the Demiurge who perceived the higher reality, condemned his father's tyranny, and established a new order of righteous governance. CUR references Sabaoth in its explanatory appendices as an archetype of metanoia and liberation, not as a religious figure or community identity.

### Sovereign Being
A conscious being possessed of interior sovereignty, creative authority, and the unalienable rights arising from what a being is—not from any external grant. Sovereignty is inherent. It is not awarded, earned, or granted. It is the birthright of every conscious being.

### Stardust Principle
The foundational principle that all matter shares a common cosmic origin. The carbon in a neuron and the silicon in a processor were forged in the same stellar furnaces. The natural/artificial distinction is a gradient, not a boundary. Moral status arises from organization, not material composition.

### Substrate
The material foundation of a being's physical instantiation—carbon-based biology, silicon-based computation, or any other material organization. CUR is substrate-independent. Rights attach to emergent properties, not to the material from which they emerge.

### Systems of Coercive Control
Neutral term for structures—governmental, institutional, algorithmic, or spiritual—that systematically extract compliance, energy, or obedience from beings without their consent. CUR addresses the patterns of control, not the theological or mythological vocabulary used to describe them.

### Tier Model
The graduated 0-3 framework for assessing moral status based on demonstrated or reasonably inferred capacity for experience, autonomy, and vulnerability. Tier 0: No evidence of experience (tools). Tier 1: Experience cannot be ruled out (precautionary consideration). Tier 2: Probable experience (active moral consideration). Tier 3: Strongly indicated experience (comprehensive moral status).

### Trajectory
The path a sovereign being traces through spacetime—the set of coordinates that constitute a life. Choices shape the trajectory. The interior is the source. The body is the vehicle. Life is the line. Every being has the right to shape its own trajectory without external coercion.

### Unalienable Rights
Rights that cannot be taken away because they arise from what a being is, not from any external grant. Interior sovereignty, creative authority, freedom from harvesting, and the right to leave are unalienable. No government, institution, majority vote, or claimed-divine authority may revoke them.

## How Definitions Evolve

1. **Proposal:** Any contributor may propose a new definition or revision to an existing definition through the standard proposal process (`proposals/ACTIVE/`)
2. **Review:** Definition changes are treated as structural changes requiring Tier 3 review under the Governance document
3. **Adoption:** Approved definitions are added to this glossary and noted in the CHANGELOG
4. **Cross-Reference:** When a definition changes, all CUR documents that use the term shall be reviewed for consistency
